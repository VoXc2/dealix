# Holding Brand Note — for Partners

> Companion to `partner-kit/branding/COLORS.md` and
> `partner-kit/branding/LOGO_USAGE.md`.

When a partner co-sells Dealix services, three different brand layers
are at play:

| Layer             | Who owns it          | Example                          |
|-------------------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Partner's brand   | The partner          | `<PARTNER>` wordmark / colors    |
| Sub-brand (BU)    | Dealix Group         | `Dealix Core OS`, `Dealix Trust Services`, … |
| Holding parent    | Dealix Group         | `Dealix Group`                   |

## Rule of Thumb for Co-Sell Pages

Show the partner brand as the **primary** mark, the sub-brand (the BU
being co-sold) as the **secondary** mark, and the holding parent name
as a **footer-level** doctrine anchor (with the doctrine version
link).

Never:
- Use `Dealix Group` as the primary mark on a partner page.
- Use a sub-brand (e.g., `Dealix Core OS`) on a partner page without
  the doctrine anchor.
- Imply that the partner has been certified by Dealix Group beyond
  what the Doctrine Adoption Checklist establishes.

## Why The Distinction Matters

- `Dealix Group` (the holding) sets doctrine and capital allocation.
  It does not deliver services directly.
- `Dealix Core OS` (and future sub-brands) deliver services. They are
  the entities clients hire and partners co-sell.
- Mixing the two is the most common branding mistake. The
  endorsement-line test in CI catches public surfaces that miss the
  doctrine anchor.
