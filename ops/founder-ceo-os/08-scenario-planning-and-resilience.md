# Scenario Planning and Resilience

## Goal
Keep Dealix strong under uncertainty.

## Scenario table

| Scenario | Signal | Risk | Response | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sales slower than expected | Meetings or close rate below target | Cash and morale pressure | Narrow ICP, improve proof, reduce spend | Founder/GTM |
| Product adoption weak | Activation below target | Retention risk | Improve onboarding and core workflow | Product/CS |
| Reliability issue | Critical incidents increase | Trust loss | Freeze new features and fix core stability | Engineering |
| Security concern | Access or data issue | Enterprise blocker | Incident review and control upgrade | Tech Lead |
| Competitor pressure | Lost deals cite competitor | Positioning risk | Update differentiation and proof | Founder/Sales |
| Funding delay | Cash runway tightens | Hiring and growth constraint | Reprioritize plan and reduce burn | Founder/Finance |

## Monthly resilience review

1. What assumption is weakest?
2. Which metric changed fastest?
3. Which risk is under-owned?
4. What would we cut first?
5. What would we protect at all costs?
6. Which customer signal matters most?

## Kill, hold, double-down rules

| Action | Use when |
|---|---|
| Kill | No evidence after defined test window |
| Hold | Valuable but blocked by dependency |
| Double down | Strong demand signal and acceptable economics |
| Rebuild | Core workflow useful but execution poor |

## Required artifacts

- Scenario register
- Risk register
- Cash trigger plan
- Product fallback plan
- Customer communication plan
- Incident postmortem template
