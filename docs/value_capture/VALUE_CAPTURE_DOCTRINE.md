# Dealix Value Capture & Monetization Architecture

> Dealix must not only create value — it must capture a fair share of that value as revenue, retainers, platform, partnership, or venture upside.

Eleventh strategic layer. Typed surfaces live in `auto_client_acquisition/value_capture_os/`.

## 1. Value Capture Ladder

```
Education → Paid Diagnostic → Sprint → Pilot → Retainer →
Managed Platform → Enterprise Program → Academy → Partners → Venture
```

## 2. Pricing philosophy

Price for **operating capability transformation**, not hours.

Reflect: problem value, data sensitivity, workflow complexity, governance level, expected proof, retainer potential, module reuse, client tier.

## 3. Offer pricing factors

- Capability Diagnostic: company size, source count, workflow count, governance level.
- Revenue Intelligence Sprint: account count, data quality, sectors, scoring depth, draft pack type, exec report level.
- Company Brain Sprint: source count, knowledge sensitivity, citation requirements, governance.
- AI Governance Review: use case count, risk level, policy count, approval matrix complexity.
- Monthly Retainer: workflows, updates, reports, support, governance, review cycles.

## 4. Retainer architecture (4 monthly OS)

- Monthly RevOps OS, Monthly Company Brain, Monthly Governance, Monthly AI Ops.

Rule: no retainer without cadence, owner, metrics, proof.

## 5. Value-based packaging — 4 tiers

- **Starter — Clarity** (Diagnostic, Data Readiness, basic Governance).
- **Growth — Operating Capability** (Sprint + Proof + drafts + dashboard).
- **Scale — Managed OS** (monthly cadence + workspace + proof timeline).
- **Enterprise — Control & Assurance** (AI Control Plane + Audit + Risk Index + SLA).

## 6. Expansion revenue map

Each offer points to the next. Revenue Intelligence → Monthly RevOps → Executive Reporting → Sales Company Brain → Workspace → Dealix Revenue OS. Similar paths from Brain, Governance, Operations.

Rule: no project without an expansion map; no proof without next commercial recommendation.

## 7. Contract structure (10 sections)

Scope, Exclusions, Client Inputs, Data Responsibility, AI Limitations, Human Approval Requirement, No Guaranteed Outcomes, Proof Pack Definition, Change Request Process, Retainer Option.

Exclusions never change: no scraping, no cold WhatsApp, no LinkedIn automation, no fake proof, no guaranteed sales claims, no unapproved external sending, no unsupported claims.

## 8. Gross margin discipline

Targets: Diagnostic 75%+, Sprint 65–75%, Pilot 55–70%, Retainer 65%+, Enterprise 50–70%, Academy 80%+, Platform 80%+ mature.

If margin is weak: raise price, reduce scope, move custom to add-on, use templates, productize a step, refuse the client.

## 9. Scope control

Define: included, excluded, inputs, review cycles, delivery format, approval owner, success metrics, change rules. Change request goes through a 6-way classification: Included / Minor / Paid add-on / New sprint / Retainer backlog / Rejected.

## 12. Upsell logic

The Proof Pack writes the upsell. Data issues → Data Readiness Retainer. Follow-up gaps → Monthly RevOps OS. Knowledge gaps → Company Brain. Policy risks → Monthly Governance. Manual reporting → Executive Reporting Automation.

## 13. Platform / Partner / Academy / Venture monetization

- **Platform** starts at 3+ retainers and after workspace pull signals (dashboard requests, repeated approvals, monthly reports).
- **Partners**: referral, advisory, implementation, certified — each with a covenant. White-label is last.
- **Academy** launches at 10+ projects, 3+ proof-backed assets, stable method, repeatable templates, QA rubric.
- **Venture** at 5+ paid clients, 2+ retainers, 10+ Proof Packs, average score ≥ 80, repeatable delivery, product module, owner, healthy margin, Core OS dependency.

## 14. Value Capture Dashboard

Revenue by offer, gross margin by offer, Revenue Quality Score, Client Quality Score, proof-to-retainer conversion, expansion revenue, platform pull signals, partner revenue, academy revenue, venture readiness, **bad revenue rejected** (the under-tracked metric).

## 15. The closing sentence

> Every Proof becomes a commercial decision — Retainer, Upsell, Product Module, higher price, Partner Opportunity, or Venture Signal — not a beautiful report that ends at delivery.
