## Context
The Operating Review Pack is the monthly artifact the leadership team
reads to make decisions about Dealix. It is the place where the daily
loop in `docs/ops/DAILY_OPERATING_LOOP.md` and the founder ops in
`docs/V14_FOUNDER_DAILY_OPS.md` meet the executive view in
`docs/EXECUTIVE_DECISION_PACK.md`.

## Sections

- **Revenue** — pipeline, won/lost, retainers, expansion, churn risk.
- **Delivery** — engagements by stage, QA scores, value claims logged.
- **Product** — AI workforce performance, eval trends, prompt and
  governance changes shipped.
- **Governance** — incidents, repeat risks, approval SLAs, audits.
- **Market** — sector signals, competitive notes, customer voice.
- **Decisions** — the decision menu pulled in from
  `docs/company/OPERATING_INTELLIGENCE.md`.

## Per-section content

Each section contains:

- 3–5 short metrics (with trend arrow).
- 1–3 narrative observations.
- A decisions list — chosen from: **sell / stop / build / publish / hire**.

## Format

- 6–10 pages maximum.
- Bilingual where audience requires.
- One owner per decision.
- One target date per decision.

## Operating cadence

- Prepared in the first week of the month.
- Reviewed in a 90-minute Operating Review on a fixed weekly cadence.
- Decisions logged and tracked to closure by the next pack.

## Anti-patterns

- Long, all-text decks with no decisions.
- Vanity metrics without business meaning.
- Decisions without owners or dates.
- Recycling the same observations month over month.

## Interfaces
| Inputs | Outputs | Owners | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ledger snapshot | Revenue / Delivery sections | Head of Delivery | Monthly |
| Control Tower data | Product / Governance sections | Head of AI | Monthly |
| Market intelligence | Market section | CEO | Monthly |
| Operating Intelligence | Decisions section | CEO | Monthly |

## Metrics
- Pack Cycle Time — days from data freeze to publication.
- Decision Closure — % of prior-month decisions completed.
- Section Coverage — % of required sections populated.
- Decision Quality — % of decisions producing expected uplift.
