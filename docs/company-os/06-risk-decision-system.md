# Risk and Decision System

## Purpose
Make founder-level decisions explicit, reversible when possible, and linked to evidence.

## Risk levels

| Level | Meaning | Required action |
|---|---|---|
| P1 | Can block launch, revenue, customer trust, or security | Owner, mitigation, weekly review |
| P2 | Can slow execution or create meaningful cost | Owner and mitigation |
| P3 | Watch item | Monitor |

## Decision types

| Type | Examples | Required rigor |
|---|---|---|
| One-way door | Pricing model, architecture, legal commitment | Formal decision record |
| Two-way door | Campaign test, UI experiment, pilot workflow | Lightweight decision record |
| Emergency | Incident response, customer escalation | Owner and postmortem |

## Founder decision checklist

Before deciding:

1. What are we optimizing for?
2. What evidence do we have?
3. What evidence is missing?
4. Is this reversible?
5. What is the cost of delay?
6. Who owns execution?
7. What would make us reverse the decision?
8. What must stop to make room?

## Weekly risk review

- New P1 risks
- Risks without owner
- Risks past trigger point
- Blocked decisions
- Decisions needing founder approval
- Decisions to revisit

## Stop rules

Stop or pause work when:

- No owner exists.
- No customer evidence exists.
- Risk is rising without mitigation.
- Cost grows without impact.
- Work conflicts with the 90-day no-list.
