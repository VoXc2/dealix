# Operating Calendar

## Purpose
Keep Dealix execution on cadence.

## Daily

| Time | Activity | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Morning | Founder daily runbook | Founder | Top 3 outcomes |
| Midday | Blocker removal | Leads | Unblocked tasks |
| Evening | Decision and risk review | Founder/Ops | Tomorrow first action |

## Weekly

| Day | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Sunday | Strategy and priorities | Top 5 weekly outcomes |
| Monday | Product and engineering | Roadmap and blocker review |
| Tuesday | GTM and sales | Pipeline movement |
| Wednesday | Customer success | Health and proof review |
| Thursday | Ops, security, AI, risk | Risk and readiness review |
| Friday | Founder memo | Weekly narrative |

## Monthly

| Week | Review | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Finance and runway | Cash and spend decisions |
| Week 2 | Product roadmap | Build/hold/stop decisions |
| Week 3 | GTM channels | Channel allocation decisions |
| Week 4 | Team and operating model | Hiring and ownership decisions |

## Quarterly

- Strategy review
- ICP review
- Moat review
- Product platform review
- AI governance review
- Security and vendor review
- Financial scenario review
- Team and leadership review

## Annual

- Company thesis refresh
- Market expansion decision
- Product platform thesis
- Capital and hiring plan
- Risk and resilience plan
- Board/advisor narrative

## Cadence rules

- If a meeting produces no decision, remove or redesign it.
- If a KPI has no owner, remove or assign it.
- If a task has no due date, it is not real.
- If a risk has no trigger, it is only a fear.
- If a launch has no rollback, it is not ready.
