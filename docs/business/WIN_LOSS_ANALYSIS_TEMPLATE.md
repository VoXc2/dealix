# Win/Loss Analysis Framework (W13.12 / W13.8)

> **Cadence:** every closed deal (win OR loss) gets a 15-min structured retro within 7 days.
> **Why:** patterns emerge after 10 wins + 10 losses. Without structured capture, lessons disappear.

## Structured Interview Questions

### For Wins

1. What problem made you start looking for a solution?
2. What other tools did you evaluate?
3. What made you choose Dealix over those alternatives?
4. What concern almost stopped you?
5. What would have made the decision easier?
6. Who else was involved in the decision?
7. On a scale 1-10, how likely are you to refer a peer?

### For Losses

1. What problem were you trying to solve?
2. What did you decide instead (competitor / build / nothing)?
3. What was the deal-breaker for Dealix specifically?
4. Was it price, features, timing, trust, or fit?
5. What would Dealix need to change for you to reconsider?
6. Who made the final no decision?
7. Permission to follow up in 6 months?

## Monthly Pattern Review

Every 1st of month, founder spends 30 min reviewing all wins/losses
from prior month. Look for:

- **Repeated win reason** → double down (sales narrative)
- **Repeated loss reason** → product/positioning gap
- **Competitor pattern** → battle card needs work
- **Sector pattern** → ICP refinement
- **Touchpoint count anomaly** → sales process tuning

## Quarterly Strategy Review

End of quarter, founder + CS lead (post-customer #10) review:

- Win rate by sector
- Win rate by competitor
- Loss reasons categorized (price/feature/trust/timing/fit)
- Action items completed vs not

## Anti-Patterns

❌ Don't survey customer immediately after close (wait 7-10 days)
❌ Don't use surveys for losses if you didn't ask permission first
❌ Don't shoot the messenger — losses are gold for product
❌ Don't aggregate without specific quotes — patterns hide in language
❌ Don't share win/loss data publicly without customer permission
