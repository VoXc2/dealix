# Commercial Sovereignty

A single revenue line is a vulnerability. Commercial Sovereignty is the discipline of operating multiple, complementary revenue streams without losing focus.

## 1. The revenue stack

- **Diagnostics** — paid qualification.
- **Sprints** — cash + proof.
- **Pilots** — operational validation.
- **Retainers** — stability.
- **Platform** — scale.
- **Academy** — authority + revenue.
- **Partners** — distribution.
- **Ventures** — upside.
- **Enterprise** — large contracts.

## 2. The role of each stream

| Stream | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Sprints | Prove |
| Retainers | Stabilize |
| Platform | Scale |
| Academy | Distribute |
| Ventures | Compound |

## 3. Operating discipline

- No single line exceeds a ceiling defined by the Intelligence OS (drift triggers review).
- Sprints must convert to retainers at the doctrine rate or the wedge is wrong.
- Platform pricing is independent of services pricing.
- Academy revenue does not subsidize delivery margin.

## 4. Pricing posture

- Price the **capability and the proof**, not hours.
- Every recurring retainer is priced above one proof item per month.
- Diagnostic is intentionally cheap; everything else is intentionally not.
- Multi-region, multi-product, multi-language as priced add-ons.

## 5. Anti-patterns

- Sliding into custom AI development with open scope.
- Pilot offers without a proof commitment.
- Hourly consulting under any brand.
- Letting one big client define the firm.

## 6. The principle

> Multi-stream revenue with a single doctrine. The streams diversify; the operating model does not.
