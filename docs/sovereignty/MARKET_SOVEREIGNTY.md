# Market Sovereignty

To own a market, own its **terms**. Market Sovereignty is the long game of category formation: when buyers, partners, and regulators all use Dealix language, the firm has won.

## 1. The sovereign terms

- Governed AI Operations
- Capability Score
- Transformation Gap
- Revenue Intelligence
- Company Brain
- Governance Runtime
- Proof Pack
- Source Passport
- AI Control Plane
- Capital Ledger
- Dealix Method

## 2. Goal

Get buyers to ask for the terms by name:

> We need a Capability Diagnostic.
>
> We need a Proof Pack.
>
> We need Revenue Intelligence.
>
> We need a Governance Runtime.

When buyers request your terminology, you are not selling inside a category — you are *making* it.

## 3. Operating discipline

- Terms are owned by the Office of the Standard.
- Every Dealix document uses the canonical terms.
- Marketing copy is checked against a glossary before publication.
- Partner and academy materials reuse the terms verbatim.

## 4. Measurement

- Category Language Adoption (see `MARKET_POWER_SCORE.md` in the global-grade layer).
- Inbound Diagnostic Requests.
- Proof Pack Requests.
- Competitor Copy Signals.

## 5. Anti-patterns

- Drift in terminology across BUs.
- Inventing new terms in marketing without updating the standard.
- Allowing competitors to redefine terms without rebuttal.
- Glossary that exists but is not enforced.

## 6. The principle

> The firm that owns the language of a category owns the category.
