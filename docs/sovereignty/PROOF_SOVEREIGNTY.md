# Proof Sovereignty

Proof must belong to Dealix as a **method**, not as a one-off marketing artifact.

## 1. Proof Pack Standard (sovereign edition)

Every Proof Pack contains:

- Problem
- Inputs
- Work Completed
- Metrics
- Before / After
- AI Outputs
- Governance Events
- Business Value
- Risks
- Limitations
- Recommended Next Step

The `Limitations` section is mandatory — claims are credible only when their boundaries are explicit.

## 2. Proof types

| Type | Example evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Revenue Proof | Pipeline value, opportunities created |
| Time Proof | Hours saved per workflow per week |
| Quality Proof | Error reduction, rework reduction |
| Risk Proof | Unsafe actions blocked, approvals logged |
| Knowledge Proof | Citation coverage, insufficient-evidence rate |

## 3. Proof rule (no exceptions)

- No proof, no claim.
- No proof, no retainer push.
- No proof, no public case.

## 4. What makes proof sovereign

Proof becomes sovereign when it has:

- A **standard format**.
- A **score** (see `command_control_os/proof_command.py`).
- An **evidence quality** dimension.
- A **governance confidence** dimension.
- An explicit **retainer linkage**.
- A **case-safe version** gated by consent and evidence level.

## 5. Operating discipline

- Reporting OS owns generation.
- Capital OS sweeps reusable assets from each pack.
- Intelligence OS reads proof scores into the decision engine.
- Governance OS contributes audit and approval evidence.
- Public cases require evidence level ≥ L4 and explicit consent.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Verbal proof in sales calls.
- Hero numbers without baselines.
- Reuse of an old pack without rechecking consent.
- Metric selection after delivery.

## 7. The principle

> Sovereign proof is reproducible, auditable, and consented. Anything else is marketing.
