# Distribution Sovereignty

Dealix is **not** founder-sales-only. Distribution Sovereignty is the discipline of opening channels with controls, not just with energy.

## 1. Channels

- Founder-led authority
- Proof-led content
- Partner referrals
- Certified implementation partners
- Academy / certification
- Enterprise trust programs
- Benchmark reports

## 2. The channel gate

A channel is opened only when **all** are true:

- Offer is clear and productized.
- Proof exists.
- QA is defined.
- Governance is enforced.
- Partner role is explicit (if applicable).

A channel opened without these creates trust debt that takes years to repay.

## 3. Partner Covenant

Every partner accepts, in writing:

- No unsafe automation.
- No guaranteed claims.
- Proof Pack required.
- QA review accepted.
- Governance rules required.
- Audit rights accepted.

A partner who violates the covenant is paused, not warned.

## 4. Channel-level controls

| Channel | Primary control |
| --- | --- |
| Founder | Proof anchoring on every claim |
| Content | Glossary + claim safety |
| Partner | Covenant + quarterly audit |
| Academy | Certification + recertification |
| Enterprise | Readiness ladder + contract addenda |
| Benchmark | Method appendix + license review |

## 5. Operating discipline

- Channels are reviewed quarterly by the Intelligence OS.
- A channel with zero inbound for two quarters is paused.
- A channel that brings risk events instead of revenue is paused.
- New channels start with a pilot scope.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Partner programs without governance.
- Content programs without proof anchors.
- Founder dependency that grows with revenue.
- Channels that exist for vanity, not for inbound.

## 7. The principle

> Open channels with controls, not with ambition.
