# Capital Sovereignty

Every engagement must feed the Capital Ledger. Capital is the sovereign asset class of the firm.

## 1. Capital types

- Service Capital
- Product Capital
- Knowledge Capital
- Trust Capital
- Market Capital
- Standard Capital
- Partner Capital
- Venture Capital

## 2. Post-engagement Capital Review

Ask, in writing:

- What asset was produced?
- Is it reusable?
- Does it raise pricing power?
- Does it shorten delivery time?
- Does it reduce risk?
- Does it raise trust?
- Does it work as content or as a benchmark?
- Does it open a retainer?

## 3. Minimum output per engagement

- 1 Trust Asset
- 1 Product or Knowledge Asset
- 1 Expansion Path

An engagement that does not produce this minimum is **strategically incomplete**, regardless of revenue.

## 4. Maturity and promotion

- 3+ reuses → candidate for productization.
- 5+ reuses → candidate for promotion to a Product Module.
- Product Module + retainer base → candidate for a venture.

The Intelligence OS proposes promotions automatically from reuse signals.

## 5. Operating discipline

- The Capital Ledger is the single source of truth.
- Every asset has an owner and a reuse count.
- Public reuse re-checks consent and evidence level.
- Cross-tenant reuse requires written consent.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Asset hoarding inside one operator’s files.
- Re-deriving the same artifact every engagement.
- Marketing an asset publicly without rechecking consent.
- Promoting assets to “productized” without QA review.

## 7. The principle

> Capital sovereignty is the difference between a firm that sells time and a company that compounds.
