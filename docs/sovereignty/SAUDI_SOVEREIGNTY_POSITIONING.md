# Saudi Sovereignty Positioning

Dealix rides the Saudi AI wave with a **clear position** that respects the national stack and adds an operating layer above it.

## 1. The public sentences

- **EN:** Saudi Arabia builds AI infrastructure. Dealix operationalizes AI inside companies.
- **AR:** السعودية تبني البنية التحتية للذكاء الاصطناعي. Dealix تشغّل الذكاء الاصطناعي داخل الشركات.

## 2. What Dealix does *not* claim

- We do not claim to compete with the national infrastructure.
- We do not claim to be a national model.
- We do not claim to replace national platforms.
- We do not claim outcomes without proof.

## 3. What Dealix does claim

- We convert national infrastructure and models into operating capability inside companies.
- We do this with runtime governance, Arabic-first quality, and Proof Packs.
- We use national open data and licensed sources under strict passport discipline.

## 4. Distribution moments

- LEAP and DeepFest are **distribution moments** for the Capability Diagnostic and the Saudi B2B AI Readiness Benchmark — not hype demos.
- Success at an event is measured in paid Diagnostic bookings, not in impressions.

## 5. Operating discipline

- In-region residency by default.
- Bilingual executive reporting by default.
- Source Passport per dataset by default.
- Regulated sectors prioritized for partnership and proof.

## 6. Why this is sovereign

Saudi Sovereignty Positioning lets Dealix expand beyond the kingdom (across MENA) without losing its credibility or its national alignment. The position is durable across changes in the national stack because Dealix *runs on top of* whatever the national stack offers.

## 7. Anti-patterns

- Marketing language that competes with national initiatives.
- Treating Arabic as a translation surface.
- Sponsoring events without measuring booked Diagnostics.
- Inferring approval from event proximity instead of from engagements.

## 8. The principle

> Sovereignty respects the national stack and adds an operating layer above it.
