# Enterprise Sovereignty

Enterprise buyers do not buy intelligence; they buy **control**. Enterprise Sovereignty is the discipline of selling at a level Dealix already operates.

## 1. The Enterprise Readiness Ladder

- **Level 1 — Trust Pack:** documented data handling, AI policy, no unsafe automation, proof standard.
- **Level 2 — Auditability:** structured audit events, AI Run Ledger, approval records, Source Passports.
- **Level 3 — Control:** policy engine, role-based approvals, Risk Index, incident response.
- **Level 4 — Enterprise Platform:** RBAC, SSO, data retention, audit exports, SLA, support process.
- **Level 5 — Enterprise AI OS:** AI Control Tower, multi-workflow governance, BU reporting, executive value dashboard.

## 2. The selling rule

> Operate Level N before you sell Level N. Every level has a real artifact behind it.

A buyer at Level 5 receives the Governed AI Operations Program with a joint operating committee. A buyer at Level 1 receives a Trust Pack — and is set up to ladder up.

## 3. Operating discipline

- The Command Center shows the firm’s current operating level and the gaps to the next.
- The Intelligence OS recommends `BUILD` decisions only when they close ladder gaps.
- BU leads sign off on their unit’s readiness at each quarterly review.
- Enterprise contracts cite the readiness level they bind.

## 4. Anti-patterns

- Selling Level 5 features when the firm operates at Level 2.
- Treating Level 4 platform features as a substitute for Level 5 control tower scope.
- Letting the ladder become aspirational only.
- Skipping the audit export discipline that Level 2 demands.

## 5. Why sovereignty matters here

A firm that promises enterprise capabilities without operating evidence is one bad reference call away from collapse. The ladder protects buyer trust and protects the firm from itself.

## 6. The principle

> Enterprise sovereignty is operating discipline expressed at contracting time.
