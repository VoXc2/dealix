# Dealix Global Command Model

> Dealix doesn’t just need a product. It needs a **command system** — a way to know what is happening, why it is happening, what the right decision is, and what must be built or stopped.

This is the third strategic layer of the doctrine. It sits on top of the endgame (constitution) and global-grade (enterprise) layers, and operates the firm in real time.

## 1. The model

```
Sense → Decide → Govern → Execute → Prove → Compound → Scale
```

| Stage | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Sense** | Capture market signals, client pain, data quality, workflow gaps, governance risks, proof events, delivery friction, partner signals. |
| **Decide** | Convert signals into structured decisions: Scale, Build, Pilot, Hold, Kill, Raise Price, Productize, Offer Retainer, Create BU, Create Venture Candidate. |
| **Govern** | Evaluate risk before execution: source, PII, allowed use, claims, channel risk, agent autonomy, approval, audit. |
| **Execute** | Ship a *productized* workflow, never ad-hoc work. |
| **Prove** | Every engagement produces a Proof Pack. |
| **Compound** | Every engagement contributes a Capital Asset. |
| **Scale** | Only scale what has proof, repeatability, and governance. |

## 2. Why this layer exists

Companies operate AI **and** the controls around it. Reports on enterprise AI adoption consistently surface two themes: governance/privacy concerns at the top of buyer risk lists, and pilots stalling without a clear strategy and ROI. The Command Model is Dealix’s answer: AI is added **inside** a command system, not on top of chaos.

## 3. The internal sentence

> We do not add AI on top of chaos. We build a command, operating, and governance system around AI.

## 4. The doctrine pairing

| Layer | Purpose |
