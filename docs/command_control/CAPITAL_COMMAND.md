# Capital Command

Every engagement must produce **capital**. Capital Command is the operating layer that ensures each project leaves a compounding asset behind.

## 1. Capital types

- **Service Capital** — checklists, SOPs, templates
- **Product Capital** — modules, scripts, dashboards
- **Knowledge Capital** — playbooks, sector patterns
- **Trust Capital** — proof packs, audits, testimonials
- **Market Capital** — content, benchmarks, partner signals
- **Standard Capital** — method, QA rubric, certification material
- **Partner Capital** — co-developed assets with named partners
- **Venture Capital** — unit playbooks and modules ready to spin out

## 2. Minimum per engagement

- 1 Trust Asset
- 1 Product or Knowledge Asset
- 1 Expansion Path

Engagements that miss this minimum are recorded as **strategically incomplete**, regardless of revenue.

## 3. Worked example — Revenue Intelligence Sprint

- **Trust Asset:** Revenue Proof Pack
- **Product Asset:** account scoring module improvements
- **Knowledge Asset:** B2B objections library for the sector
- **Market Asset:** anonymized insight for the sector benchmark
- **Expansion Path:** Monthly RevOps OS

## 4. Operating discipline

- The Capital Ledger is the single source of truth.
- Assets carry an owner and a reuse count.
- Promotion to Product Module happens after reuse thresholds, not on intuition.
- Public reuse re-checks consent and evidence level.

## 5. Quarterly rituals

- **Capital Review** — retire stale assets, promote ripe ones.
- **Productization Sprint** — convert top assets into Core OS modules.
- **Method Update** — fold accepted patterns into the Dealix Method.

## 6. Failure modes

- Asset hoarding inside one operator’s files.
- Re-deriving the same artifact every engagement.
- Promoting assets to “productized” without QA review.
- Sharing assets across tenants without consent.

## 7. The principle

> Without Capital Command, Dealix is a labor shop with AI tools. With it, Dealix is a compounding company.
