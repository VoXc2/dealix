# Saudi Trust Layer

Dealix is **Saudi-aware by design**. The Saudi Trust Layer captures the data, language, and channel disciplines that make Dealix legible to local regulators, executive buyers, and partners.

## 1. Layer contents

- Arabic business QA
- PDPL-aware data handling
- Source Passport with residency
- Consent / relationship status
- WhatsApp sensitivity rules
- No cold outreach automation
- Bilingual executive reporting

## 2. Defaults

- Data residency in-region unless explicitly waived.
- Personal data redacted before any external transit.
- WhatsApp is never used for cold outreach.
- All executive reports are produced in Arabic and English at the same time.
- Public claims require a Proof Pack ID.
- Engagements default to draft-only outputs.

## 3. Regulatory alignment

NDMO is the national regulator for data management and protection in the Saudi public sector. Dealix builds its data governance against NDMO-aligned principles by default — even for engagements outside the public sector — because that bar protects the firm and the buyer.

## 4. Operating discipline

- Every engagement’s data handling is documented in the Source Passport(s).
- Every outbound channel is checked against the WhatsApp / SMS / email rule pack at runtime.
- Consent records are first-class objects, not free-text notes.
- Bilingual outputs are reviewed by an Arabic-native voice.

## 5. The trust sentence

> We do not put AI on unclear data. We start with source, allowed use, sensitivity, and governance — then AI.

## 6. Productization

- **Saudi Trust Brief** — a one-page artifact shipped with every engagement.
- **PDPL-aware checklist** — used during diagnostic.
- **Arabic disclosures pack** — boilerplate for executive reports.
- **Channel risk rule pack** — WhatsApp / SMS / call sensitivity rules.

## 7. Failure modes

- Treating the trust layer as a marketing line instead of an artifact.
- Defaulting to English-only reporting in Arabic-led organizations.
- Cold outreach automation under any branding.
- Sharing engagement data across tenants without explicit consent and aggregation.
