# Dealix Strategic Risk, Compliance & Resilience System

> Dealix becomes strong not only because it sells and builds, but because it knows what can break it and prevents the break before it happens.

Layer 20 consolidated doctrine. Typed surfaces in `auto_client_acquisition/risk_resilience_os/`.

## 1. Risk philosophy

> Risk is not a blocker. Risk is a design input.

When risk appears (PII, scraping request, cold WhatsApp, unsupported claim, agent autonomy, source-less answer, partner overpromise, weak proof) — convert it into a Rule / Test / Checklist / UI status / Sales boundary / Trust Pack update / Governance Runtime decision.

## 2. Risk taxonomy (12 categories)

1. Data Risk
2. Privacy Risk
3. AI Output Risk
4. Agent Autonomy Risk
5. Channel Risk
6. Claim Risk
7. Client Risk
8. Delivery Risk
9. Partner Risk
10. Financial Risk
11. Market Risk
12. Strategic Drift Risk

Each risk has owner, severity, likelihood, control, early-warning signal, response plan, and an associated test/checklist.

## 3. Data Risk

Source Passport + Allowed Use Registry + PII Detection + Retention Policy. Rules: no passport = no AI; unknown source = block external use; PII + unclear basis = redact or block; external action = approval required.

## 4. Privacy Risk

Privacy enforced at runtime, not in marketing. PII redaction before AI calls; no PII in logs; minimum-necessary data; Privacy Runtime Board; audit trail; retention limits.

## 5. AI Output Risk

Output schema validation, human review, QA score, citation requirement for Company Brain, unsupported claim detector, Arabic Business QA. Tiers: ≥90 client-ready, 80–89 review, 70–79 revise, <70 reject.

## 6. Agent Autonomy Risk

Agent Card + Agent Registry + Autonomy Levels + Allowed Tools + Forbidden Actions + Audit Log + Approval Queue + Decommission Rule. MVP allows 0–3 only.

## 7. Channel Risk

Draft-only by default. External action approval. Consent / relationship status. No cold WhatsApp automation. No LinkedIn automation. No scraping engine.

## 8. Claim Risk

No proof = no claim. Estimated/Observed/Verified classification. Public cases require Verified.

## 9. Client Risk

Score per client. High-risk client → paid diagnostic only. Unsafe request → reject or reframe. No owner → no sprint. No data clarity → Data Readiness first.

## 10. Delivery Risk

Service catalog + scope boundaries + delivery checklist + QA rubric + review cycles + change request system. Every custom request becomes scope, paid add-on, or rejected.

## 11. Partner Risk

Partner Covenant + Certification + QA audits + Approved messaging + Audit rights + Suspension policy.

## 12. Financial Risk

Revenue Quality Score + Bad Revenue Filter + Capital Allocation Score + Gross margin by offer + Capital assets per project. Revenue that destroys focus is debt.

## 13. Market Risk

Market Power Dashboard + Category Language Tracking + Benchmark Reports + Content Engine + Offer Win Rate + Objection Tracking.

## 14. Strategic Drift Risk

Constitution + Non-negotiables + Board Memo + Kill System + Capital Allocation Board + Strategic Bets Framework. If drift appears: freeze new features, review offers, kill weak services, return to Revenue Intelligence + Governance + Proof.

## 15. Resilience System — 5 shocks

Client incident, Model provider change, Partner violation, Market slowdown, Governance failure. Each has a runbook.

## 16. Risk Register

Typed in `risk_resilience_os.risk_register`. Each entry: ID, owner, likelihood, impact, early warning, control, response.

## 17. Saudi Risk Layer

PDPL-aware data handling, Arabic claim safety, WhatsApp boundary, human approval, source transparency, bilingual proof.

## 18. Risk Dashboard

Open risks, high-severity risks, governance incidents, blocked unsafe actions, PII flags, partner violations, bad revenue rejected, scope creep incidents, weak proof count, agent permission risks.

## 19. The closing sentence

> Dealix wins when risks become fuel for system improvement: each risk becomes a rule, each incident a test, each objection a policy, and each expansion only happens when proven, governed, and auditable.
