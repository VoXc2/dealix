## Context
Custom-work agencies cannot compound. They sell hours, not assets, and
every project resets the clock. These five rules exist to keep Dealix
away from that gravity well. They sit on the Intelligence Layer because
they bind every Core OS decision — what to accept, what to deliver, what
to close. See `docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md` and
`docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md` for the strategic foundation, and
`CAPITAL_ALLOCATION_SCORE.md` for the score that enforces them.

## The five rules

### 1. No custom work without productization review
Every custom request enters a productization review before scoping. If
the work cannot articulate a reusable module, a templated workflow, or a
market-safe insight as an output, it is declined. The review is run by
the Head of Product against the Capital Ledger and Productization Ledger.

### 2. No project closes without a capital asset
A project cannot be marked closed until at least one entry exists in the
Capital Ledger tied to that project — a script, a dataset, a prompt, a
playbook update, a benchmark, or a market-safe insight. The Delivery
team owns this. The CFO refuses to recognise revenue closure without it.

### 3. No service scales without a repeatable checklist
A service line cannot be invested in (Scale band per
`CAPITAL_ALLOCATION_SCORE.md`) until its delivery checklist is templated,
versioned, and used in at least three projects without modification. The
checklist lives in `docs/memory/delivery_lessons.md` and the Capital
Ledger.

### 4. No client work without a proof pack
Every client engagement must produce at least one proof event of measured
value before invoicing the final milestone. Proof events are recorded in
the Proof Ledger with proof type (Revenue Value / Time Saved / Risk
Reduced / Decision Quality), metric, value, and client approval. No proof
event, no closure, no case study, no reference.

### 5. No feature without repeated demand
A feature candidate cannot move from proposed to building in the
Productization Ledger until demand is documented across at least three
distinct paid projects. Single-client features become custom code; that
is the agency trap. The Head of Product is accountable for refusal.

## Interfaces
| Inputs | Outputs | Owners | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Custom request intake | Productization review verdict | Head of Product | Per request |
| Project close events | Capital asset confirmation | Head of Delivery | Per close |
| Service line scoring | Checklist enforcement | Head of Delivery | At scale decision |
| Proof Ledger | Pre-invoice check | Head of Revenue | Per milestone |

## Metrics
- **Rule 1 compliance** — share of accepted custom work with a passed
  productization review (target: 100%).
- **Rule 2 compliance** — share of closed projects with a Capital Ledger
  entry (target: 100%).
- **Rule 3 compliance** — share of Scale-band lines with a templated
  checklist used in >=3 projects (target: 100%).
- **Rule 4 compliance** — share of engagements with at least one client-
  approved proof event (target: 100%).
- **Rule 5 compliance** — share of building-stage features with demand
  across >=3 projects (target: 100%).
