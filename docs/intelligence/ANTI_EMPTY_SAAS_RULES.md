## Context
The opposite trap to the agency trap is premature SaaS — building modules,
self-serve flows, billing engines, marketplaces, and enterprise controls
before there is proven, paid, repeated value. Empty-SaaS bleeds cash,
distracts engineering, and creates a brittle product narrative that the
market does not believe. These five rules force Dealix to earn its SaaS
posture rather than declare it. See
`docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`,
`docs/DEALIX_V3_AUTONOMOUS_REVENUE_OS.md`, and
`docs/OFFER_LADDER_AND_PRICING.md` for the staged commercialisation path.

## The five rules

### 1. No SaaS module before internal usage
A module cannot be exposed as a SaaS feature until it has been used
internally across at least three paid projects and recorded in the
Productization Ledger with a `released` status. Internal usage is the
only honest pilot.

### 2. No self-serve before assisted value is proven
No client-facing self-serve flow ships until assisted delivery of the
same outcome has produced proof events for at least three clients (per
the Proof Ledger). Self-serve scales failure as fast as success — only
proven outcomes deserve scaling.

### 3. No billing engine before repeatable retainers
A productised billing engine — usage metering, plans, invoicing
automation — is not built until Dealix has at least three live retainers
priced from the same packaging in `docs/OFFER_LADDER_AND_PRICING.md`. The
retainer is the contract that justifies the engine.

### 4. No marketplace before assets are proven
A partner or template marketplace is not opened until at least five
reusable assets have been used in production with attribution and
measurable outcomes in the Capital Ledger and Proof Ledger. A marketplace
of unproven assets damages brand more than it earns revenue.

### 5. No enterprise controls before enterprise demand
Enterprise-grade controls — SSO, advanced RBAC, custom audit exports,
private deployment — are not built until at least two paying enterprise
customers have signed contracts requiring them. The Customer Success and
Revenue teams must produce written intent, not anticipated demand.

## Interfaces
| Inputs | Outputs | Owners | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Productization Ledger | Module release gate | Head of Product | Per release |
| Proof Ledger | Self-serve readiness check | Head of Product + Revenue | Per launch |
| Active retainers | Billing engine go/no-go | CFO + Head of Product | Quarterly |
| Capital + Proof Ledgers | Marketplace go/no-go | CEO | Per proposal |
| Signed enterprise contracts | Enterprise build gate | CEO + Head of Sales | Per request |

## Metrics
- **Rule 1 compliance** — share of public modules with >=3 internal paid
  projects in the Productization Ledger (target: 100%).
- **Rule 2 compliance** — share of self-serve flows backed by >=3
  assisted proof events (target: 100%).
- **Rule 3 compliance** — billing engine work only with >=3 live retainers
  recorded (target: 100%).
- **Rule 4 compliance** — marketplace launches only with >=5 proven
  reusable assets (target: 100%).
- **Rule 5 compliance** — enterprise features built only against signed
  enterprise demand (target: 100%).
