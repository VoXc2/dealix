# Dealix CEO Operating Rhythm & Execution Council

> Strategy doesn't execute itself. Dealix needs an Operating Rhythm that turns every week into decisions, every month into Proof, and every quarter into a stronger strategic bet.

Layer 23 consolidated doctrine. Typed surfaces in `auto_client_acquisition/operating_rhythm_os/`.

## 1. Execution Council — 8 roles

CEO/Strategist, Revenue Owner, Delivery Owner, Product Owner, Governance Owner, Proof Owner, Client Success Owner, Finance/Capital Owner. Early on, one person holds many — but the **roles** exist.

## 2. Weekly Operating Meeting — 10-section agenda

Top 3 CEO Decisions, Revenue Pipeline, Active Delivery, Proof Packs, Governance Risks, Client Adoption, Productization Signals, Capital Allocation, Stop/Kill List, Commitments.

Mandatory outputs: 3 decisions, 3 commitments, 1 risk to reduce, 1 proof to strengthen, 1 thing to stop.

> If the meeting ends with no "thing to stop", you are over-expanding.

## 3. Daily CEO Check (10 minutes)

- Top revenue opportunity today?
- Top delivery risk today?
- Top governance risk today?
- Proof to improve today?
- Thing not worth your time today?

> Do not start the day from inbox. Start from CEO decisions.

## 4. Decision Queue

```json
{
  "decision_id": "DEC-001",
  "type": "BUILD",
  "target": "Approval Center MVP",
  "evidence": ["approval friction across 4 clients"],
  "owner": "Product Owner",
  "deadline": "this_week",
  "decision_status": "pending"
}
```

Rule: every decision has evidence; every repeated evidence without a decision is waste.

## 5. Weekly Proof Review

Score ≥ 85 → case candidate; ≥ 80 + adoption ≥ 70 → retainer; < 70 → delivery improvement; repeated pattern → benchmark.

## 6. Weekly Governance Review

All AI runs logged? All outputs governance-statused? PII flags? Claims rejected? Unsafe automation requested? Agent autonomy escalation? Incidents?

## 7. Weekly Productization Review

Repeated 1–2 times → template; 3+ → script/internal tool; 5+ with pull → client feature; cross-retainer → platform module.

## 8. Monthly Board Memo (12 sections)

Executive Summary, Revenue Quality, Proof & Value, Governance & Incidents, Client Adoption, Productization, Capital Assets, Market Intelligence, BU Maturity, Stop/Kill Decisions, Capital Allocation, Next Strategic Bets.

## 9. Quarterly Strategic Review

Bigger questions: best wedge, governance product readiness, workspace pull, BU readiness, services to kill, market language adoption, price increases, partner need, hire need, venture candidate.

Outputs: 1 strategic bet + 1 service to scale + 1 service to kill + 1 product module + 1 risk reduction + 1 narrative push.

## 10. Scorecard Stack

Weekly = execution truth. Monthly = business truth. Quarterly = strategic truth.

## 11. Bad Revenue Council

Weekly: list deals to reject (scraping, cold WhatsApp, guaranteed sales, unclear data, open scope, weak margin, no proof, no retainer). Reframe, reprice, refer out, or refuse.

> Dealix does not refuse money; it refuses revenue that destroys trust and focus.

## 12. Hiring Rhythm

Triggers per role: Delivery Analyst (3+ repeated projects), Product Engineer (5+ repeated manual steps with productization queue), Customer Success (2+ retainers), Governance Reviewer (enterprise demand), Partner Manager (3+ active partners).

> No hire before playbook. No delegation without QA rubric.

## 13. The closing sentence

> Dealix wins when it doesn't depend on enthusiasm — but on an execution rhythm: weekly decisions, monthly Proof, quarterly strategic bets, and daily focus on good revenue, governance, and the assets that make Dealix stronger.
