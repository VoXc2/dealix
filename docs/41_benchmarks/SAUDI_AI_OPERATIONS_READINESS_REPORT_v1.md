## 1. Why this report exists — لماذا هذا التقرير

Saudi B2B operations are adopting AI at speed. Most of that adoption is silent: employees pasting client data into public chat tools, managers forwarding model-generated drafts unreviewed, projects quietly automating outreach with no record. Across sectors, the gap is not between "AI" and "no AI" — it is between governed AI use and ungoverned AI use.

This report introduces an open framework for measuring that gap. It defines five readiness dimensions, scores them on a synthetic dataset shaped to mirror what we observe qualitatively across five sectors, and gives organizations a way to assess and lift their own readiness without disclosing client-identifiable data.

The framework is open. The standards it references — Source Passport, Runtime Governance, Proof Pack, Agent Control — are published in `docs/23_standards/`.

## 2. Methodology — المنهجية

### Data source

- **Synthetic and aggregated.** The dataset is generated by Dealix internally from a parameterized model that encodes the readiness dimensions and applies sector-specific priors derived from qualitative observation (workshops, public posts, partner conversations, sector reports).
- **No real client data.** No client account, contract, dataset, or identifying field contributes to the numbers below.
- **K-anonymity ≥ 5.** Whenever a slice would resolve fewer than five synthetic organizations, the slice is suppressed.

### Scoring

Each synthetic organization is scored on five readiness dimensions, each from 0 to 100. The overall readiness score is the simple arithmetic mean of the five dimensions. There is no proprietary weighting; the formula is fully disclosed.

### Disclosure

The construction parameters, distributions, and sector priors are listed in section eight. A future v2 will incorporate self-reported assessment data submitted through an opt-in form, with the same k-anonymity floor and the same suppression rules.

### What this is not

This report does not rank companies. It does not name organizations. It is not a market sizing study. It is a structural benchmark for self-assessment.

ليس هذا التقرير ترتيباً للشركات، ولا تسمية لأي جهة، ولا دراسة لحجم السوق. هو إطار قياس للتقييم الذاتي.

## 3. The five readiness dimensions — أبعاد الجاهزية الخمسة

| Dimension | Question it answers |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Leadership alignment** | Does the executive team agree on what AI is for, where it is allowed, and who owns it? |
| **Workflow clarity** | Are the AI-affected workflows written down with named owners and named steps? |
| **Data readiness** | Are inputs identified, owned, and labeled with Source Passport-style metadata? |
| **Human capability** | Do the people executing the workflows know how to read, review, and override AI outputs? |
| **Governance maturity** | Are external-bound outputs passed through a recorded decision, with an audit trail? |

A short bilingual gloss:

- **مواءمة القيادة** — هل يتفق التنفيذيون على دور الذكاء الاصطناعي وحدوده ومالكه؟
- **وضوح المسار** — هل المسارات المتأثرة مكتوبة بأصحاب وخطوات معروفة؟
- **جاهزية البيانات** — هل المدخلات مُعرَّفة ومملوكة وموسومة؟
- **قدرة الإنسان** — هل المنفِّذون قادرون على مراجعة المخرجات وتجاوزها عند اللزوم؟
- **نضج الحوكمة** — هل المخرجات الخارجية تمر بقرار مسجَّل وأثر قابل للتدقيق؟

## 4. Synthetic findings — النتائج الاصطناعية

> All numbers in this section are SYNTHETIC and AGGREGATED. Treat them as illustrative of the framework, not as a measurement of any real population.

### 4.1 Overall readiness distribution

Across the synthetic population, overall readiness scores cluster heavily below 60. The distribution is right-skewed: most organizations are in the Siloed or Structured maturity range, with a narrow tail at the higher end.

| Score band | Maturity equivalent | Share of synthetic population |
|------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| 0 – 29 | Siloed | 22% |
| 30 – 49 | Structured | 38% |
| 50 – 69 | AI-Assisted | 27% |
| 70 – 84 | Governed | 11% |
| 85 – 100 | Orchestrated | 2% |

### 4.2 By sector

Aggregated patterns for five Saudi B2B sectors. Each cell is the average score on that dimension for that sector in the synthetic dataset.

| Sector | Leadership | Workflow | Data | Human | Governance | Overall |
|--------|------------|----------|------|-------|------------|---------|
| B2B services | 56 | 49 | 41 | 52 | 38 | 47 |
| Training and education | 51 | 44 | 38 | 58 | 34 | 45 |
| Logistics | 48 | 52 | 44 | 39 | 36 | 44 |
| Real estate services | 44 | 38 | 32 | 41 | 28 | 37 |
| Healthcare administration | 53 | 47 | 49 | 46 | 51 | 49 |

Observations from the synthetic patterns:

- **Governance is the weakest dimension across all five sectors.** This is consistent with the qualitative pattern: AI is used, but decisions about its outputs are not recorded.
- **Healthcare administration leads on governance** in the synthetic priors because regulatory exposure forces some form of recorded decision-making, even when it is informal.
- **Real estate services scores lowest overall** in the synthetic dataset; the sector's reliance on personal networks and informal outreach corresponds to weak passport coverage and weak workflow clarity.
- **Training and education scores highest on human capability** but lowest on workflow clarity; individual capability is not the same as operationalized capability.
- **B2B services sits near the median** on every dimension, which is why it serves as the worked example in [b2b_services_sample.md](../sector-reports/b2b_services_sample.md).

## 5. What separates Saudi-AI-Ready companies — ما يميِّز الشركات الجاهزة

From the synthetic dataset, five patterns separate organizations scoring above 70 from those scoring below 50.

1. **A named workflow owner per AI-assisted process.** Not a team, not a tool. One person whose job description includes the workflow.
2. **A passport per source.** Every dataset, file, channel, or feed used by AI carries a Source Passport that names the owner, allowed use, sensitivity, and retention rule.
3. **A recorded decision on every external output.** No output crosses a customer-facing boundary without a recorded `governance_status` value from the seven-decision vocabulary.
4. **A Proof Pack at every project close.** Not at every "big" project — at every project. The pack is the artifact that converts the work into evidence.
5. **A published operating cadence.** Daily, weekly, monthly. The team knows when reviews happen, who attends, and what gets decided.

The pattern is structural, not technological. None of the five requires a specific model, a specific vendor, or a specific tool.

النمط بنيوي وليس تقنياً. لا يستلزم أي من العناصر الخمسة موديلاً معيناً ولا أداة بعينها.

## 6. How to lift your score — كيف ترفع درجتك

Concrete actions, ordered by leverage. Each item maps to an open standard you can implement without buying any specific product.

### 6.1 Issue a Source Passport for your top ten data sources

Identify the ten datasets or feeds your team uses most. For each, fill in the nine required fields defined in [SOURCE_PASSPORT_STANDARD.md](../23_standards/SOURCE_PASSPORT_STANDARD.md). This single action typically lifts the Data dimension by 15-25 points in the synthetic model.

### 6.2 Name a Workflow Owner for every AI-assisted process

Write the workflow down. List its steps. Assign a person. Publish the name. This typically lifts the Workflow dimension by 10-20 points.

### 6.3 Adopt the seven-decision governance vocabulary

Even without runtime tooling, you can implement the decisions on paper: every external-bound output gets a one-line decision record before it ships. See [RUNTIME_GOVERNANCE_STANDARD.md](../23_standards/RUNTIME_GOVERNANCE_STANDARD.md). This is the highest-leverage move; governance is the weakest dimension across sectors.

### 6.4 Close every project with a Proof Pack

Use the fourteen-section schema in [PROOF_PACK_STANDARD.md](../23_standards/PROOF_PACK_STANDARD.md). The first one is hard; the second is easier; the third becomes routine.

### 6.5 Publish your operating cadence

A daily standup, a weekly review, a monthly Proof Pack assembly. Written down. Followed. This is the cheapest action with the largest durability premium.

## 7. Limitations — حدود التقرير

- **Synthetic data only.** No real organization contributed numbers. Real-world distributions may differ in magnitude even where the structural patterns hold.
- **Sector coverage is narrow.** Five sectors do not cover the Saudi B2B landscape. Public sector, financial services, and industrial operations are not modeled in v1.
- **K-anonymity ≥ 5 enforced.** Sub-sector slices may be suppressed where the synthetic sample is too small.
- **Self-assessment dependence in v2.** Future versions that incorporate opt-in self-assessment will inherit reporting bias; that bias will be disclosed in the next methodology section.
- **Not a regulatory framework.** This report describes operational readiness. It does not measure compliance with the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law or sectoral regulations.

## 8. Construction parameters — معامِلات البناء

For full transparency, the synthetic dataset is generated as follows:

- Population: 1,000 synthetic organizations across five sectors with sector-weighted size distributions.
- Dimension priors: per-sector mean and standard deviation set from qualitative observation; the values used are listed in the table in section 4.2.
- Correlation structure: governance is positively correlated with leadership and workflow; data readiness is positively correlated with workflow. Within-organization noise is normal with standard deviation 8.
- Aggregation: cells below k=5 synthetic organizations are suppressed; no cell in this report falls below that threshold.

These parameters will be re-disclosed and updated in v2 alongside any opt-in self-assessment data.
