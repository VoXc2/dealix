# Road to Enterprise AI OS

> Enterprise is not a starting offer. Enterprise is the **outcome of
> earned readiness** — a customer accumulates capability levels and
> proof, and only then is the Enterprise AI OS contract appropriate.

## The 6-step path

Per `docs/enterprise/ENTERPRISE_DECISION.md`, no Enterprise contract is
signed without the gate; this doc shows the path that *opens* that gate.

### Step 1 — Diagnostic (SAR 7,500–25,000, 5–10 days)
Capability Assessment across all 7 capabilities. Output: capability scorecard + recommended first Sprint + governance/data risk map.

### Step 2 — First Sprint (SAR 9,500–20,000, 7–21 days)
Build the first capability from Level 1 to Level 3. Deliver Proof Pack. Customer experiences Dealix's Delivery Standard + 5-gate QA.

### Step 3 — Pilot (SAR 22,000–45,000, 30 days)
Operate the new capability inside the customer's business for 30 days. Capture before/after numbers. Capability moves from Level 3 to Level 4.

### Step 4 — Managed OS / Retainer (SAR 15,000–60,000 / mo, ongoing)
Recurring monthly cadence. Continuous improvement of one capability. NRR ≥ 100% within 6 months. Capability moves toward Level 5.

### Step 5 — Expand to additional capabilities
Customer signs additional Sprints for adjacent capabilities. Typical sequence: Revenue → Reporting → Knowledge → Operations → Governance. Each new capability follows the same Level-1→5 progression.

### Step 6 — Enterprise AI OS
Once Enterprise Decision gate is met (10+ Sprints, 3+ retainers, 0 governance incidents, audit-export live, RBAC designed, SLA template signed), upgrade to Enterprise AI OS:

- **Setup**: SAR 85,000–300,000+.
- **Monthly**: SAR 35,000–150,000.
- Multi-team, multi-capability, governed platform with SLA + audit + dedicated CS + procurement-ready contracts.

## What the customer experiences across the journey

| Stage | Customer feeling | Dealix presence |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|
| Diagnostic | "These guys think clearly" | 2 calls + report |
| Sprint | "We just got a working output" | Weekly updates |
| Pilot | "This is actually changing how we operate" | Bi-weekly review |
| Retainer | "Dealix is part of how we run" | Monthly OS report |
| Expansion | "We should be using them for X too" | Quarterly business review |
| Enterprise | "Dealix is the AI layer of our company" | Embedded operating partner |

## Gates between stages (no skipping)

| From → To | Gate |
|-----------|------|
| Diagnostic → Sprint | Customer signs SOW within 30 days |
| Sprint → Pilot | Quality Score ≥ 85; Customer satisfied with Proof Pack |
| Pilot → Retainer | 30-day ROI demonstrable; recurring need present |
| Retainer → Expand | Customer health ≥ 80 for 3 months; additional capability gap identified |
| Expand → Enterprise | Enterprise Decision gate fully met (`ENTERPRISE_DECISION.md`) |

## Anti-pattern: signing Enterprise too early

Don't do it. Signs you're being pushed too fast:
- "Let's just do everything at once."
- "Skip the Sprint, give us the full platform."
- "We'll figure out the SOW later."
- "Send the contract first, we'll align on scope after signing."

Response: paid Diagnostic, then Sprint. The customer who insists on skipping is the customer who will fail your project and damage your reputation.

## Why the road, not the leap

Customers who follow the road:
- Build trust at each step (every Sprint produces a Proof Pack).
- Internalize the Dealix Method (each step trains them in the operating model).
- Justify the Enterprise price through earned ROI.
- Become the loudest reference customers (because they remember the journey).

Customers who try to leap to Enterprise:
- Buy AI without operational readiness → project fails → trust collapses.
- Drain founder time on bespoke scope → margins die.
- Become "we tried Dealix and it didn't work" stories → reputation hit.
