# Capability Assessment

Use **before** scoping a sprint: seven capabilities × current vs target level. Evidence must be **client-grounded** (artifacts, interviews, exports).

| Capability | Current Level | Target Level | Evidence | Recommended service |
|------------|---------------:|-------------:|----------|---------------------|
| Revenue | 1 | 3 | leads in spreadsheet | Lead Intelligence Sprint |
| Customer | 1 | 3 | WhatsApp overload | AI Support Desk Sprint |
| Operations | 0 | 2 | no workflow map | AI Quick Win Sprint |
| Knowledge | 1 | 3 | files scattered | Company Brain Sprint |
| Data | 1 | 3 | duplicates / missing fields | Data Readiness Assessment (→ cleanup sprint) |
| Governance | 0 | 2 | no AI policy | AI Governance Program / readiness |
| Reporting | 1 | 3 | manual executive reports | Reporting / exec pack (per catalog) |

## Decision rule

```text
Highest gap + highest value + lowest risk = first sprint
```

If **data** or **governance** risk is high, start with **readiness** or diagnostic—not full automation ([`RUNTIME_GOVERNANCE.md`](../../governance/RUNTIME_GOVERNANCE.md)).
