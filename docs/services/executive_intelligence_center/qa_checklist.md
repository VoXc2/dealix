# QA Checklist

Run before any signal, decision memo, or weekly brief reaches the executive.

## Signals

- [ ] Each signal traces to a named, dated data source
- [ ] No estimated figure is presented as verified — labelled "estimated"
- [ ] Thresholds match the calibration agreed at setup
- [ ] False-positive rate reviewed; noisy signals suppressed or re-tuned
- [ ] No revenue or proof figure is fabricated (gate: no_fake_revenue, no_fake_proof)

## Decision memos

- [ ] States the decision needed and the recommended option clearly
- [ ] Lists the evidence behind each option, with sources
- [ ] States assumptions and what could change the recommendation
- [ ] Marked advisory — the executive approves; nothing is auto-executed
- [ ] No legal, CFO-level, or external-representation advice included
- [ ] Routed through the approval queue, not actioned directly

## Weekly executive brief

- [ ] One page or less; lead with what changed and what needs a decision
- [ ] Signals, opportunities, and risks each have a source reference
- [ ] No marketing language; concrete nouns and numbers only
- [ ] Open approval items listed with owners and due dates
- [ ] Time-saving commitment status noted vs baseline
- [ ] Reviewed by the delivery lead before release
