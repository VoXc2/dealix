## Context
The contractually binding scope of the AI Governance Program. The Program is multi-phase and substantial — it must avoid sprawl. References the strategic plan in `docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`, the runtime governance regime in `docs/governance/RUNTIME_GOVERNANCE.md`, and the enterprise controls matrix in `docs/enterprise/CONTROLS_MATRIX.md`.

## Duration
- **4–12 weeks** end-to-end, sized to company complexity.
- Kickoff within 10 business days of signed SOW + deposit.

## In Scope
1. **AI inventory** — up to 30 AI tools / use cases catalogued.
2. **Data flow mapping** for each inventoried use.
3. **Lawful basis assessment** for each dataset.
4. **Risk register** — top 30 AI risks ranked and owned.
5. **Approval matrix** — up to 40 AI use case patterns mapped.
6. **Audit process** — quarterly + annual templates and walk-through.
7. **Controls matrix** — alignment with `docs/enterprise/CONTROLS_MATRIX.md`.
8. **Training package** — exec, owner, builder, user roles.
9. **Policy updates** — up to 5 existing policies revised (AI Usage, Data Handling, Vendor, Incident, Approval).
10. **Proof pack** + executive proof report.

## Not In Scope
- **More than 30 AI uses** inventoried in the Program. Larger orgs = scoped engagement.
- **Legal filings** with regulators.
- **Certification audits** (ISO 27001 / SOC 2). We prepare; certification body certifies.
- **Penetration testing** of systems.
- **Vendor due-diligence reports** beyond what is needed for the matrix.
- **Translation** of policies to languages beyond AR + EN.
- **Long-term operation.** Operation is **Monthly Governance** retainer.
- **Mediation** between client and any regulator.
- **Custom system integration** of governance tooling.

## Assumptions
1. The client provides a **list of AI tools and processes in use** within 10 business days of kickoff.
2. The client provides **data flow descriptions** (or accepts a 2-hour workshop to draft them).
3. The client provides **existing policies** (or signals "we don't have one" — we draft from scratch).
4. The client provides **incident history** (or attests to none in writing).
5. The client provides **lawful basis statements per dataset** (or works with us to derive them).
6. The client engages their own counsel for any regulatory filing.
7. The client agrees to the DPA (`docs/DPA_DEALIX_FULL.md`) and the cross-border posture.

## Dependencies
- AI tool list by end of Week 1.
- Data flow descriptions by end of Week 2.
- Existing policy export by end of Week 2.
- Incident history by end of Week 2.
- Counsel-of-record nominated by end of Week 1.

## Change Control
- Inventory additions up to 5 above scope → no fee.
- Beyond 5 additions → re-scope.
- Policy additions beyond the agreed 5 → +SAR 4,000 per policy.

## Geography & Language
- Delivery in Arabic + English.
- Other languages on request, +SAR 3,000/lang.
- PDPL-aware. Multi-jurisdiction (GCC + non-GCC) carries a +SAR 8,000–20,000 premium.
- ZATCA and sector-specific (healthcare, finance, education) overlays available at +SAR 5,000–15,000.

## Acceptance
The Program is accepted when:
1. AI inventory delivered.
2. Risk register, approval matrix, controls matrix delivered.
3. Audit process documented.
4. Training package delivered.
5. Sponsor signs handoff note.

Auto-acceptance after 10 business days of silence.
