## Context
The employee guide translates the AI policy into plain language a non-technical employee can follow on a busy day. The aim is for any colleague to know — within 60 seconds of opening this guide — whether what they are about to do with an AI tool is OK, needs approval, or is forbidden. It supports the policy template in `./policy_template.md` and is part of the offer in `./offer.md`. It aligns with the spirit of `docs/DPA_DEALIX_FULL.md`, `docs/DATA_RETENTION_POLICY.md`, and `docs/ops/PDPL_RETENTION_POLICY.md`.

## How to use this guide
- Read it once when you join, and again when the policy is refreshed.
- Bookmark it in your tools.
- When you are unsure, ask your manager. When still unsure, do not use AI for that task.

## What is OK
You can use approved AI tools to:

- **Draft** internal documents, summaries, agendas, and meeting notes from approved internal information.
- **Summarize** approved documents and public sources.
- **Brainstorm** ideas, outlines, options, and pros/cons lists.
- **Translate** internal documents within approved tools.
- **Explain** concepts, code, formulas, and processes.

In all cases, you are responsible for what you send out. AI helps you draft; you review and decide.

## What needs approval
The following require manager approval and may also require Governance approval:

- **Customer-facing output** generated or substantially shaped by AI — emails to clients, marketing copy, public posts.
- **Automation that takes external actions** — bots that send messages, post content, or call third-party APIs.
- **Use of a new AI tool** that is not on the approved list — even free or "personal" tools.
- **Uploading internal documents to AI tools** that go beyond the basic approved scope.

When in doubt, ask before sending.

## What is forbidden
You must not:

- **Upload sensitive data to public chatbots** — national IDs, banking details, health information, customer PII, M&A plans, HR exits, salary data.
- **Fabricate data** using AI — invent customer feedback, financials, citations, or quotes.
- **Take autonomous external actions** — let an AI tool send messages, file forms, or transact with third parties without explicit human approval.
- **Present AI-generated work as fully human-authored** where regulation, contract, or [Client] policy requires disclosure.
- **Use unapproved AI browser extensions** on company devices or accounts.

## If you make a mistake
Mistakes happen. The faster a mistake is reported, the smaller the harm.

1. Stop using the tool for the task.
2. Notify your manager.
3. Capture what was shared (with whom, when, what).
4. Your manager notifies [Owner] and follows the incident response process.

There is no penalty for reporting a mistake quickly. There is a penalty for hiding one.
