## 3. Case Study Program

### 3.1 Case Study Eligibility

**Requirements:**
- [ ] Client health score 80+
- [ ] Value proof L3+ (verified)
- [ ] Specific, measurable results
- [ ] Client willing to participate
- [ ] No ongoing disputes
- [ ] At least 90 days engagement

### 3.2 Case Study Types

| Type | Named | Permission | Use |
|------|-------|------------|-----|
| Named Case Study | Yes | Explicit written | Full marketing |
| Anonymized Case Study | No | Implied | Marketing, sales |
| Testimonial | Optional | Written | Website, proposals |
| Metrics Only | No | None | Internal use |

### 3.3 Case Study Rules

**Required:**
- ✅ Accurate data (no exaggeration)
- ✅ Client permission (written for named)
- ✅ Evidence level stated
- ✅ Real outcomes only
- ✅ Honest about limitations

**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Fake client outcomes
- ❌ Exaggerated ROI
- ❌ Misleading statistics
- ❌ Unverified claims
- ❌ Permission bypass
- ❌ Named use without consent

## 5. Advocacy Process

### 5.1 Advocacy Pipeline

```
Stage 1: Advocacy Identified
  └─> Client shows advocacy signals
  └─> Document in CRM
  └─> Add to advocacy queue

Stage 2: Advocacy Qualified
  └─> Assess eligibility
  └─> Determine advocacy type
  └─> Identify ask

Stage 3: Advocacy Requested
  └─> Approach client
  └─> Make specific request
  └─> Handle objections

Stage 4: Advocacy Secured
  └─> Collect testimonial/permission
  └─> Create case study draft
  └─> Get client approval

Stage 5: Advocacy Activated
  └─> Publish/use content
  └─> Track performance
  └─> Thank client
```

### 5.2 Approval Flow

```
Client agrees to advocacy
       ↓
Draft content created
       ↓
Client reviews and approves
       ↓
Written permission obtained
       ↓
Content published
       ↓
Client credited (if named)
```

## 9. Advocacy Guidelines

### 9.1 Best Practices

- ✅ Ask at the right moment (after success)
- ✅ Be specific about what you're asking
- ✅ Make it easy for the client
- ✅ Always get written permission
- ✅ Always give credit
- ✅ Always thank the client
- ✅ Track everything

### 9.2 Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Asking too early (before value proven)
- ❌ Being pushy
- ❌ Using content without permission
- ❌ Misrepresenting client situation
- ❌ Exaggerating results
- ❌ Forgetting to thank
- ❌ Losing track of permissions
