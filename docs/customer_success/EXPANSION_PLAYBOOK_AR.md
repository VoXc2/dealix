## 4. Expansion Process

### 4.1 Phase 1: Expansion Identification

**Goal:** Identify expansion opportunities

**Activities:**
- Review weekly reports for signals
- Ask discovery questions in calls
- Monitor stakeholder changes
- Track new use cases mentioned
- Watch for department expansion

**Expansion Signals Checklist:**
- [ ] Client mentions new problem area
- [ ] Other team asks about services
- [ ] Executive wants "more of this"
- [ ] Client asks about capabilities
- [ ] New budget cycle approaching
- [ ] Competitor losing to us

### 4.2 Phase 2: Expansion Qualification

**Goal:** Validate expansion opportunity

**Activities:**
- Understand new use case fully
- Identify decision makers
- Assess budget availability
- Evaluate competitive position
- Determine timeline

**Qualification Questions:**
- "What would success look like for this new area?"
- "Who else is affected by this problem?"
- "What's the timeline for addressing this?"
- "What's the budget for solving this?"
- "Who makes the final decision?"

### 4.3 Phase 3: Expansion Proposal

**Goal:** Create compelling expansion proposal

**Proposal Must Include:**
1. Delivered value summary
2. Evidence level (L3+ required)
3. New use case solution
4. Implementation plan
5. Investment required
6. Expected outcomes
7. Risk assessment
8. Timeline
9. Required approvals

### 4.4 Phase 4: Expansion Pitch

**Goal:** Close the expansion

**Activities:**
- Present proposal
- Address objections
- Negotiate terms
- Get approval
- Execute expansion

## 6. Expansion Pricing

### 6.1 Pricing Guidelines

| Expansion Type | Base Pricing | Adjustment |
|----------------|-------------|------------|
| Same workflow extension | Standard | 0% |
| New workflow (same team) | Standard | 0-10% |
| New team/department | Standard | 10-20% |
| Full OS upgrade | Standard | 15-25% |
| Custom development | Custom | Case-by-case |

### 6.2 Value-Based Expansion

Expansion price should reflect:
- Additional value created
- Reduced implementation cost (existing relationship)
- Competitive advantage
- Relationship strength

## 9. Expansion Anti-Patterns

### 9.1 Don't Do

- ❌ Push expansion before value proven
- ❌ Expand into unrelated use cases
- ❌ Expand without stakeholder buy-in
- ❌ Underprice expansion deals
- ❌ Promise without proof
- ❌ Expand at risk of relationship

### 9.2 Do

- ✅ Time expansion with success
- ✅ Build on existing strength
- ✅ Get executive endorsement
- ✅ Price for value delivered
- ✅ Prove with evidence
- ✅ Protect core relationship
