# Dealix Funding Memo

## Stage
Bootstrapped, pre-revenue at the time of writing.
Path designed to reach Invoice #1 before any external capital is required.

## Thesis
Saudi and GCC companies are adopting AI faster than they can govern, measure, and operationalize it. Dealix sells the missing operating layer — governed AI operations — as a productized service, not a generic automation tool.

## Capital Posture
Dealix prefers proof before capital.
External capital, if raised, is to:
- accelerate delivery repeatability
- harden evidence infrastructure
- expand partner distribution
- harden security and audit readiness

External capital is explicitly NOT used to:
- launch broadly across the GCC before Saudi proof
- fund a full SaaS platform before paid services demand
- fund vanity hiring
- fund autonomous external AI agents
- fund any motion that violates the 11 non-negotiables

## Sizing (Indicative, Not a Commitment)
- Bootstrapped runway target: reach Invoice #1, then 3 paying retainers.
- Angel / pre-seed indicative range: 1.5M – 3M SAR.
- First milestone for capital: 10 governed-ops retainers active.

## Risk and Discipline
Dealix is designed to be verified, not merely trusted. The same doctrine that protects buyers protects investors:
- public Dealix Promise API
- doctrine tests in CI
- Source Passport before AI use
- Governance Runtime before client-facing output
- Proof Pack before public claims

## Open Items For Investor Conversation
- Saudi commercial milestones before GCC expansion
- Partner-led versus direct distribution mix
- Retainer-to-sprint conversion rate evidence
- Capital Asset Library compounding metrics
