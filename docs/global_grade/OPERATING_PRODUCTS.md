# Operating Products

Stop thinking in features. Think in **operating products**. An operating product is a unit of capability that an operator (or operator-team) runs over time.

## 1. Internal operating products

- Data Readiness Engine
- Governance Runtime
- Proof Pack Generator
- Capital Ledger
- Founder Command Center
- Productization Ledger

These are the products the firm operates **for itself**. They are how Dealix runs Dealix.

## 2. Client operating products

- Client Workspace
- Capability Dashboard
- Approval Center
- Proof Timeline
- Value Dashboard

These are products the client interacts with as part of an engagement.

## 3. Enterprise operating products

- AI Control Plane
- Audit Exports
- Policy Registry
- Agent Registry
- AI Run Ledger
- Risk Index

These are gated by the Enterprise Readiness Ladder (`ENTERPRISE_READINESS_LADDER.md`).

## 4. Ecosystem operating products

- Partner Portal
- Academy Portal
- Certification Tracker
- Benchmark Reports

These power distribution, authority, and partner enablement.

## 5. Rules

- Every operating product has a named owner.
- Every operating product has a KPI tied to a Business Unit or to the firm.
- Operating products are versioned, not rebuilt.
- Operating products show up in the Capital Ledger.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Treating internal tools as “scripts” instead of as products.
- Letting client-facing products drift in branding and UX.
- Building enterprise products before the ladder gates them.
- Ecosystem products that exist without ecosystem activity to power them.

## 7. Why this framing matters

A firm that ships features grows linearly. A firm that ships **operating products** that operators run compounds — because every product carries policy, telemetry, capital, and proof along with its functionality.
