# Capital Ledger — Enterprise View

Extends `docs/endgame/CAPITAL_LEDGER_STRATEGY.md`. Same ledger, additional rules for enterprise engagements and ventures.

## 1. Capital types

- **Service Capital** — checklists, SOPs, templates
- **Product Capital** — modules, scripts, dashboards
- **Knowledge Capital** — playbooks, sector patterns, objection libraries
- **Trust Capital** — proof packs, audits, testimonials
- **Market Capital** — content, benchmarks, partner signals
- **Standard Capital** — method, QA rubric, certification material
- **Venture Capital** — unit playbooks and product modules ready to spin out

## 2. Minimum per engagement

- 1 Trust Asset
- 1 Product or Knowledge Asset
- 1 Expansion Path

Engagements that miss this minimum are marked **strategically incomplete**, regardless of revenue.

## 3. Enterprise rules

- An asset created from regulated client data inherits the client’s sensitivity and license.
- Cross-tenant reuse of any asset requires written consent.
- Aggregate benchmarks may use anonymized data with explicit aggregation rules.
- Public reuse requires L4+ evidence and explicit consent.

## 4. Compounding

- Reuse count is the canonical metric of asset maturity.
- 3+ reuses → candidate for productization.
- 5+ reuses → candidate for promotion to a Product Module.
- Product Module + retainer base → candidate for venture (`docs/endgame/VENTURE_FACTORY.md`).

## 5. Quarterly rituals

- **Capital Review** — audit ledger, retire stale assets, promote ripe ones.
- **Productization Sprint** — convert top assets into Core OS modules.
- **Method Update** — fold accepted patterns into the Dealix Method.

## 6. Failure modes

- Assets hoarded inside one operator’s files.
- Re-deriving the same artifact for every client.
- Marketing an asset publicly without rechecking consent.
- Promoting assets to “productized” without QA review.

## 7. Why this is the difference between a firm and a company

A firm sells time. A company sells **time + compounding capital**. The Capital Ledger is the difference between “Dealix did a project” and “Dealix gained an asset.”
