# Market Power Score

A single composite signal of how the market is moving around Dealix. The score is intentionally built from **category-formation indicators**, not vanity metrics.

## 1. Indicators

- Category Language Adoption
- Inbound Diagnostic Requests
- Partner Referrals
- Proof Pack Requests
- Benchmark Downloads
- Academy Waitlist
- Enterprise Trust Inquiries
- Content-to-Call Conversion
- Competitor Copy Signals

## 2. Interpretation

- **Proof Pack requests rising** — trust has become a product.
- **Capability Score requests rising** — category language is spreading.
- **Partner certifications requested** — distribution is forming.
- **Enterprise trust inquiries rising** — trust maturity is recognized.
- **Competitor copy** — the category is being shaped.

## 3. Composite

Each indicator is rated **sustained** or **not sustained** for a quarter. The Market Power Score is the count of sustained indicators. The doctrine triggers escalation only when at least three indicators are sustained for two consecutive quarters.

## 4. Anti-vanity discipline

Dealix does not track:

- Follower counts.
- Likes / impressions.
- Awards not tied to proof.

These metrics may exist for the founder voice — they do not enter the Market Power Score.

## 5. Operating discipline

- The score is reviewed quarterly by the Intelligence OS.
- Escalations in market spend require the score plus a corresponding capital plan.
- Drops in the score are investigated as seriously as drops in revenue.

## 6. Failure modes

- Adding indicators because they are easy to collect.
- Confusing one-quarter spikes with sustained indicators.
- Letting marketing redefine “sustained” to fit campaigns.
- Treating the score as a target rather than a diagnostic.

## 7. The endgame check

Dealix wins on Market Power when buyers, partners, and regulators all use Dealix language for the category — and Dealix is the firm they reach for when a capability must be operable, governed, and provable.
