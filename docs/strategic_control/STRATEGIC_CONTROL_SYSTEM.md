# Strategic Control & Market Leadership System

> Dealix does not chase the AI market. Dealix leads the definition of **governed AI operations** for Saudi and Gulf companies.

This is the fifth strategic layer of the doctrine. It sits on top of the endgame, global-grade, command-and-control, and sovereignty layers, and answers a different question: *not "what do we build?" but "what do we control, what becomes a standard, and where do we lead?"*

## 1. The model

```
Sense → Control → Execute → Prove → Compound → Lead
```

| Stage | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Sense** | Capture market and customer signals: repeated problems, repeated objections, unsafe AI requests, weak data, proof requests, retainer requests, partner opportunities. |
| **Control** | Decide what must pass through Governance, QA, Proof, Capital Ledger, and the Productization Gate. |
| **Execute** | Only execute work that has a buyer, pain, scope, proof path, governance boundary, and retainer path. |
| **Prove** | Every engagement ends in a Proof Pack. |
| **Compound** | Every engagement produces a Capital Asset. |
| **Lead** | Assets become standards, academy material, partner methods, benchmarks, business units, ventures. |

## 2. The leadership question set

A strong firm asks:

- What do we need to **control**?
- What do we need to *not* depend on?
- What needs to become a **standard**?
- What needs to become a **product**?
- What needs to become a **business unit**?
- What needs to **stop immediately**?

If a leadership review does not produce structured answers to all six, the review failed.
