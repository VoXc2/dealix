# No Weak Scale Rule

Do not scale anything weak. Scale magnifies the underlying state — including its failures.

## 1. Do not scale a Service if

- Proof is weak.
- QA is unstable.
- There is no retainer path.
- There is no governance enforcement.
- Margin is thin.
- It is not repeatable.

## 2. Do not scale a Partner if

- They do not understand the Dealix Method.
- They do not accept QA.
- They sell guaranteed-outcome claims.
- They bypass governance.

## 3. Do not scale a Product if

- It has not been used internally.
- It does not save time.
- It is not tied to revenue.
- It does not reduce risk.

## 4. Do not scale a Market if

- The buyer is unclear.
- Budget is consistently weak.
- Data risk is high.
- The sales cycle exceeds engagement size.

## 5. Operating discipline

- Every proposed scale-up requires a written check against this list.
- A `SCALE` decision in the Decision Log must cite which check it passed.
- Failures trigger `HOLD` or `KILL` decisions, not negotiation.
- The Intelligence OS surfaces scale candidates with their weakness scores attached.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Scaling because revenue allows it.
- Scaling because operators are bored.
- Scaling to justify a previous investment.
- Scaling to outrun a competitor without proof.

## 7. The principle

> Scaling weakness produces stronger weakness. Strengthen first; scale second.
