# Market Command Dashboard

A small dashboard for **category formation**. It does not duplicate the Command Center's operational views — it measures whether Dealix is leading.

## 1. Required indicators

- Category Language Adoption
- Inbound Diagnostics
- Proof Pack Requests
- Partner Leads
- Enterprise Trust Inquiries
- Benchmark Downloads
- Academy Interest
- Competitor Copy Signals

## 2. Why these eight

Market leadership shows up when the market reaches for Dealix language, methods, and artifacts — not when revenue rises. These indicators are the early evidence of category formation.

## 3. Interpretation

- **Category Language Adoption rising** — terminology is spreading.
- **Inbound Diagnostics rising** — paid entry is functioning.
- **Proof Pack Requests rising** — the trust mechanism has become a product.
- **Partner Leads rising** — distribution is forming.
- **Enterprise Trust Inquiries rising** — runtime governance is recognized.
- **Benchmark Downloads rising** — authority is compounding.
- **Academy Interest rising** — the method is being adopted by operators.
- **Competitor Copy Signals rising** — the category is being shaped.

## 4. Decision rule

Escalate market investment when at least three indicators are sustained for two consecutive quarters. See `auto_client_acquisition/endgame_os/market_power.py` and `auto_client_acquisition/global_grade_os/market_power_score.py`.

## 5. Operating discipline

- The dashboard is reviewed quarterly.
- Indicators added without a decision log entry are removed.
- Vanity metrics (followers, impressions, awards) are explicitly excluded.
- A drop in any indicator triggers a `HOLD` decision until investigated.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Treating the dashboard as marketing reporting.
- Replacing indicators with whatever rose last quarter.
- Including channels that are not actually open.
- Letting partner leads dominate the dashboard at the expense of inbound.

## 7. The principle

> Market leadership is sustained, not announced.
