# Board Memo Template

A monthly memo that converts the firm from a project executor into a holding-company leader. The template is fixed. Sections are filled in every month, even when the answer is "none."

```
# Dealix Board Memo — <YYYY-MM>

## 1. What did we sell?
## 2. What did we deliver?
## 3. What did we prove?
## 4. What risks did we block?
## 5. What assets did we create?
## 6. What repeated enough to productize?
## 7. Which client is ready for retainer?
## 8. Which service should scale?
## 9. Which service should stop?
## 10. Which market signal matters?
## 11. Which unit is maturing?
## 12. What is the next strategic bet?
```

## 1. Why this matters

A monthly memo with these twelve questions:

- Forces leadership to think across the whole firm, not only this month's deliverables.
- Surfaces stop and scale decisions before they become emergencies.
- Builds an internal record that compounds with the Capital Ledger.
- Aligns founders, BU leads, and the Office of the Standard on one page.

## 2. Drafting discipline

- Drafted by the founder or COO; reviewed by BU leads.
- Numbers cite the Decision Log and the Proof Library.
- Every "stop" recommendation cites a kill criterion.
- Every "scale" recommendation cites the six conditions.

## 3. Anti-patterns

- Memos written to look impressive.
- Stop sections that say "none" every month.
- Strategic bets that change every month.
- Memos that drift from the template.

## 4. The principle

> A board memo is leadership in writing. Write it monthly, even when nobody outside the firm asks for it.
