# Strong Scale Rule

Scale when **six conditions** hold simultaneously.

## 1. The six conditions

- **Proof** — strong, signed Proof Packs across multiple engagements.
- **Repeatability** — delivery has become a checklist.
- **Margin** — healthy gross margin.
- **Governance** — runtime checks active and used.
- **Demand** — repeat requests from buyers.
- **Owner** — a named person willing to lead the scaled unit.

## 2. Worked example — Revenue Intelligence

Eligible to scale when:

- The sprint is repeatedly bought.
- The proof score is high.
- Delivery is a checklist.
- Buyers ask for monthly follow-up.
- `account_scoring` and `draft_pack` are repeatedly reused.
- Governance checks fire and resolve correctly.

When the six conditions hold:

- Raise price.
- Build a Product Module.
- Author a Playbook.
- Offer the recurring Retainer.
- Recruit a Partner.

## 3. Operating discipline

- A `SCALE` decision cites the six conditions and the evidence for each.
- The Intelligence OS pre-populates the evidence rows from the metrics engine.
- Scale moves include a capital plan (operator hours, ledger entries to promote).
- Scale moves include a kill-criteria addendum in case the move underperforms.

## 4. Why all six matter

- Proof without repeatability is luck.
- Repeatability without margin is a labor trap.
- Margin without demand is a forecasting error.
- Demand without governance is a trust hazard.
- An owner without the other five is a hero burnout in waiting.

## 5. Anti-patterns

- Scaling on five conditions out of six and "we’ll figure out the last one."
- Letting a strong proof carry the rest of the decision.
- Promoting an operator to lead without a written brief.
- Scaling before the playbook exists in the Capital Ledger.

## 6. The principle

> Scale is earned by simultaneous strength, not by one heroic dimension.
