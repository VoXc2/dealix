# Enterprise Control Narrative

For the enterprise buyer, lead with **risk** and resolve with **control**.

## 1. Open the conversation

> Your AI risk is not that employees use AI. Your risk is unmanaged AI execution: unclear data sources, unlogged model runs, unapproved outputs, unsafe external actions, and no proof of value.

**AR:** مخاطركم ليست فقط أن الموظفين يستخدمون AI. المخاطر في التشغيل غير المحكوم: مصادر بيانات غير واضحة، تشغيل نماذج غير مسجل، مخرجات بلا موافقة، إجراءات خارجية غير آمنة، ولا يوجد proof للأثر.

## 2. Resolve with the operating control layer

> Dealix provides the operating control layer: Data Readiness, Governance Runtime, AI Run Ledger, Human Approval, Proof Pack, and Executive Value Reporting.

## 3. What the buyer hears

- A diagnosis of the actual risk surface.
- A named operating layer they can ask their team about.
- A list of artifacts they will receive.
- A measurable proof endpoint.

## 4. What the buyer does not hear

- Generic “AI strategy.”
- Tool comparisons.
- Vendor positioning against national initiatives.
- Outcome claims without proof.

## 5. Operating discipline

- The narrative is used identically in slides, proposals, and contracts.
- Every claim ladders to a Proof Pack ID or a Control Plane component.
- Arabic and English versions are produced together.
- Updates to the narrative go through the Office of the Standard.

## 6. Failure modes

- Drift in the narrative across BUs.
- Adding marketing flourishes that exceed the underlying evidence.
- Customizing the narrative per deal until it loses its shape.
- Letting sales agree to commitments outside the narrative.

## 7. The principle

> Open with risk. Resolve with control. Close with proof.
