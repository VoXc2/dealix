# Market Leadership Stack

To lead a market, you operate seven layers — in order. Skipping a layer leaks authority.

## 1. The seven layers

1. **Language** — the words the market uses.
2. **Method** — how Dealix produces outcomes.
3. **Proof** — the unit of evidence.
4. **Trust** — the artifacts buyers ask for.
5. **Benchmarks** — public, sourced, periodic.
6. **Distribution** — multiple channels with controls.
7. **Standards** — the rules a category lives by.

## 2. Language

## 3. Method

The Dealix Method is the named sequence:

```
Diagnose → Design → Build → Govern → Validate → Deliver → Prove → Operate → Compound
```

Public, periodic, sourced — anchored to the Proof Library:

- Saudi B2B Data Readiness Benchmark

National events such as LEAP and DeepFest are used as distribution moments for these benchmarks — not as hype.

## 9. Operating discipline

- Glossary edits go through the Office of the Standard.
- Method changes carry a version and a recertification trigger.
- Benchmarks ship with a method appendix.
- Channels open only after passing the channel gate.

## 10. The principle

> The firm that owns the words, the method, and the standards owns the category.
