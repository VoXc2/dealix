# Proof as a Governance Artifact

The Proof Pack is not only a sales tool. It is a **governance artifact** — the evidence record for what happened, why, who approved, and what was blocked.

## 1. Required sections (institutional)

- Problem
- Inputs
- Source Passports
- Work Completed
- Metrics
- Before / After
- AI Outputs
- Governance Events
- Blocked Actions
- Business Value
- Risks
- Limitations
- Recommended Next Step

`Source Passports`, `Blocked Actions`, and `Limitations` are non-negotiable additions over the basic Proof Pack.

## 2. Proof Score (institutional weights)

| Component | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Metric clarity | 20 |
| Evidence quality | 20 |
| Source clarity | 15 |
| Governance confidence | 15 |
| Business relevance | 15 |
| Retainer linkage | 15 |

Total 100. Each component is rated 0–100; the score is the weighted average.

## 3. Decision tiers

```
85+        → case candidate
70–84      → sales support
<70        → internal learning only
```

## 4. The hard rules

- No proof, no claim.
- No proof, no public case.
- No proof, no retainer push.

## 5. Operating discipline

- Reporting OS owns generation.
- Capital OS sweeps reusable assets from each pack.
- Intelligence OS reads proof scores into the decision engine.
- Governance OS contributes the audit and approval evidence.

## 6. Anti-patterns

- Verbal proof in sales calls.
- Hero numbers without baselines.
- Reuse without rechecking consent.
- Selecting metrics after delivery to look favorable.

## 7. The principle

> Proof is governance in human-readable form. Anything else is marketing.
