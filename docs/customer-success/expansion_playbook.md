# Expansion Playbook

## 1. Context

Expansion is where the Saudi GTM thesis becomes a business. Acquisition gets a logo; expansion earns the right to compound. The math is unambiguous: at our pricing structure, a Year-1 logo at SAR 240K that grows to SAR 720K by end of Year-2 produces lifetime value that pays back acquisition cost three times over and funds the next vertical entry. Without expansion, we are running a logo treadmill.

This playbook codifies the triggers, motions, and rules of engagement for expansion across the four CS segments. It is consumed by named CSMs (Enterprise, Sovereign), pooled CSMs (Growth), and Sales (AEs run the multi-year and AR partner co-sell motions in lockstep with CS). The playbook is QBR-driven — expansion conversations happen at scheduled cadence, not ad hoc — and is grounded in customer-verified outcome data.

The northstar metric is expansion ARR attach: ≥ 35% of Year-1 cohort produces incremental ARR within 12 months. The mechanism is utilization-driven, not concession-driven: when the customer is consuming what they bought and producing measurable outcomes, the expansion conversation is a continuation, not a sale.

## 2. Audience

- **Named CSMs (Enterprise, Sovereign)** — run all five expansion motions; QBR-driven cadence.
- **Pooled CSMs (Growth)** — surface expansion-qualified leads to AEs; do not close.
- **AEs** — close multi-year and high-value seat / vertical expansions; co-own with CSM.
- **HoCS + CRO** — joint approval on multi-year discounts and AR partner co-sell terms.
- **Customer's economic buyer + executive sponsor** — the buying coalition for expansion.

## 3. Decisions / Content

### 3.1 Expansion Triggers

Expansion conversations are triggered by signals, not by sales calendar. Five canonical triggers:

| Trigger | Signal source | First mover | Default motion |
|---------|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| **80% value-metric utilization** (3 consecutive months) | Health dashboard | CSM | Seat expansion or value-metric upsell |
| **100% value-metric utilization** (one month) | Health dashboard | CSM + AE | Value-metric upsell (urgent) |
| **New BU / subsidiary** | CSM relationship intelligence; org-chart watch | Named CSM | Seat + BU expansion |
| **Vertical expansion** (customer enters a new buyer vertical) | QBR discussion; Decision Passport telemetry | Named CSM + Vertical Lead | Vertical expansion |
| **Geographic expansion** (customer expands into UAE, Egypt, Kuwait) | QBR; M&A signals | Named CSM + AE + AR partner | GCC co-sell motion |

A trigger does not auto-pull the trigger on a conversation — it queues the expansion play for the next QBR or, for the 100% utilization case, an immediate side-meeting. We never ambush the customer with a quote; we ground every expansion in their outcome data.

### 3.2 Motion: Seat Expansion

Seats expand when the customer's team grows or a new BU is onboarded. Default mechanic:

1. **Diagnostic** — confirm new headcount and use-case fit; not every new hire warrants a seat.
2. **Trial pack** — 14-day shadow access for up to 3 candidate users (no extra fee).
3. **Commercial** — convert at the per-seat list price; multi-seat block discount of 10% at 10+ seats, 20% at 25+.
4. **Provisioning** — within 48 hours of contract amendment; bilingual onboarding artifacts issued.

Seat expansion is the most predictable motion and the lowest friction. It is also the most common: roughly 50% of expansion ARR in Year-2 forecasts comes from seats.

### 3.3 Motion: Vertical Expansion

A customer who started in one vertical (e.g., manufacturing buyer) decides to also pursue another (e.g., construction). The unit economics shift — different ICP data, different exclusion rules, different output cadence.

1. **Scoping call** — Vertical Lead joins; ICP validation; data-source mapping.
2. **Calibration pilot** (mini) — 500 enriched leads from the new vertical at no incremental cost, over 14 days.
3. **Commercial** — vertical attach fee SAR 60K + per-vertical incremental enriched-lead pricing.
4. **Onboarding** — 30-day adoption cycle reduced from the new-logo onboarding because operational scaffolding already exists.

The mini-pilot is the discipline — we calibrate before we contract. This is the same logic as the new-logo paid pilot, scaled down because the customer is already proven.

### 3.4 Motion: Value-Metric Upsell

Customer is consuming all contracted enriched leads. Three sub-motions:

- **Pack expansion** — buy an additional contracted pack at the per-lead price ladder.
- **Tier migration** — move from Growth to Enterprise tier (more leads, more seats, named CSM if not already).
- **Revenue-share migration** — for Sovereign and select Enterprise, migrate from fixed-pack to a hybrid contract with a smaller fixed base + revenue-share component on attributable pipeline above a threshold.

The revenue-share migration is reserved for accounts where (a) we have verified outcome data over two full QBRs, (b) the customer's RevOps will share attribution telemetry in real-time, and (c) the multi-year framing is on the table. The math has to work for both sides; if it does not, we keep the fixed-pack structure.

### 3.5 Motion: Multi-Year Renewal

A renewal conversation is also an expansion conversation. The multi-year play is a structured upgrade applied at the renewal window (Day -90 to Day -30 before renewal).

1. **Outcome verification** — last 12 months of attributable pipeline / revenue restated and signed by customer's CFO.
2. **Multi-year framing** — 2-year at 8% discount on Year-1 list; 3-year at 15% discount on Year-1 list; with annual escalator capped at the lower of CPI or 5%.
3. **Expansion bundling** — pull in seat expansion, vertical expansion, and / or AR partner co-sell into the multi-year amendment to reset the relationship at the higher ACV.
4. **Approval gates** — 2-year: HoCS sign-off; 3-year: HoCS + CRO + CEO sign-off.

Multi-year is the single biggest lever on NRR. A 3-year contract at 15% discount with a CPI escalator and a bundled expansion typically produces lifetime ARR 2.5–3.0x the annual baseline.

### 3.6 Motion: GCC Co-Sell with AR Partners

Customers expanding from Saudi into UAE, Egypt, Kuwait, or Bahrain trigger our partner co-sell motion. We do not direct-deliver outside Saudi in 2026; we deliver through AR partners (see `docs/partnerships/`).

1. **Partner selection** — match customer to a vetted AR partner in the target geography per the partner registry.
2. **Joint deal team** — Dealix AE + Dealix CSM + partner AE + partner delivery lead.
3. **Commercial structure** — Dealix retains the IP licensing fee + 60% of expansion ARR; partner takes 40% + delivery margin.
4. **Customer-facing** — single Dealix-fronted contract with partner named as approved sub-processor; customer's experience is one team.

The AR partner motion is what makes GCC expansion capital-light. We do not need to stand up local entities in every market; we extend our reach through partners while retaining the IP and the customer relationship.

### 3.7 QBR-Driven Cadence

All expansion motions are scheduled into QBRs (see `cs_framework.md` §3.5). The QBR agenda Section 4 ("Expansion conversation, 15m") is the canonical entry point.

- **QBR -2 (60 days before)** — CSM prepares expansion thesis from utilization + outcome data.
- **QBR -1 (30 days before)** — CSM aligns with AE; HoCS pre-approves commercial framing.
- **QBR (0)** — expansion presented to customer; verbal alignment captured; written follow-up within 48 hours.
- **QBR +30** — contract amendment circulated; close.

The off-cycle exception is 100% utilization in a single month — that triggers an immediate side-meeting because the customer is operationally unable to continue, and pretending otherwise damages trust.

### 3.8 Anti-Patterns

- **Concession-led expansion** — leading with a discount to manufacture an expansion. Banned.
- **Expansion without outcome verification** — proposing more leads when the customer has not converted the existing ones. Banned.
- **Expansion at the wrong level** — pitching multi-year to a day-to-day owner without the economic buyer present. Re-route.
- **Out-of-scope geographic direct delivery** — promising direct delivery in UAE without partner. Banned per §3.6.
- **Unbundled multi-year** — using multi-year as a discount mechanism without bundling expansion. Inefficient — re-bundle.
