# Dealix Operating Finance & Capital Allocation System

> Dealix doesn't only need a growth plan. It needs a **capital allocation system**: what deserves investment, what deserves to be stopped, who deserves hiring, what deserves productization, and what must be refused even when it brings money.

Eighteenth strategic layer. Typed surfaces live in `auto_client_acquisition/operating_finance_os/`.

## 1. Capital allocation thesis

> Invest only where learning compounds.

Dealix invests in: offers that sell repeatedly, workflows that become retainers, features that reduce delivery cost, governance that reduces risk, proof that increases trust, partners that increase distribution, standards that increase authority, ventures emerging from proof.

Dealix does **not** invest in: custom work without repetition, features without usage, clients who reject governance, segments without a clear buyer, unsafe automation, content without a category purpose, partnerships without QA.

## 2. Eight investment buckets

1. Revenue Capture
2. Delivery Efficiency

## 4. Spend rules

- No feature before repeated workflow pain.
- No growth spend before offer-message-proof fit.
- No hire before role playbook.
- No SSO/RBAC/Audit before real enterprise demand.
- No Academy before stable Method + proof-backed assets.
- No Venture without paid clients, retainers, proof library, owner.

## 5. Budget allocation by stage

Stage 1 — Founder Proof; Stage 2 — Repeatable Sprint; Stage 3 — Retainer Engine; Stage 4 — Enterprise Trust; Stage 5 — Ecosystem; Stage 6 — Venture Factory. Each stage has a focus list; do not jump ahead.

## 6. Engineering allocation order

Core OS reliability → Governance & audit → Proof generation → Delivery efficiency → Client Workspace → Enterprise controls → Partner/Academy → Advanced automation.

Engineering rule: build the thing that reduces repeated operational pain.

## 7. Hiring triggers

1st bottleneck Delivery → Delivery Analyst. 2nd Productization → Product Engineer. 3rd Retainers → Customer Success / AI Ops Operator. 4th Governance → Governance Reviewer. Do not hire Growth early; if the offer is unclear, Growth scales the chaos.

## 8. Financial control metrics

Revenue by offer, gross margin by offer, delivery hours by offer, AI cost by workflow, QA/review hours, scope-creep incidents, proof score by offer, retainer conversion, capital assets per project, **bad revenue rejected**.

Two most important: gross margin by offer + capital assets per project.

## 9. Bad revenue filter

Reject when: scope open, margin weak, risk high, client rejects governance, scraping requested, guaranteed sales requested, no proof path, no retainer path, no productization signal.

Rule: revenue that destroys focus is debt.

## 10. Opportunity cost

Every yes is a no. Before accepting a project ask: does this prevent us from building a more important asset? does the client create useful proof? is the sector strategic? does the scope become a playbook? is the revenue good or noisy?

## 11. Capital Review Meeting

After every project, 10 questions: revenue / margin / proof / asset / feature candidate / governance rule / sales insight / client expansion / pricing decision / repeat offer. Mandatory outputs: Capital Asset, Productization Candidate, Expansion Recommendation, Pricing Note, Risk Note.

## 12. Saudi capital allocation angle

Invest in Arabic executive outputs, Saudi sector taxonomy, PDPL-aware Source Passport, Revenue Intelligence, AI Governance Review, Proof Packs, Client Workspace. Do not invest in model training, data center infra, generic AI assistant, mass scraping tools, consumer apps.

## 13. Agent capital discipline

Invest in Agent Cards, Agent Registry, Audit Logs, Approval Queue, Policy Engine, Tool Permissions. Do not invest in autonomous external outreach, self-directed sales agents, unapproved CRM actions, multi-agent execution without audit.

## 14. The closing sentence

> Dealix wins not by building everything but by knowing what not to build, who not to serve, which revenue to reject, and where to put capital so every project turns into an asset, every asset into a platform, and every platform into a scalable unit.
