# Value-Based Pricing Metrics

## 1. Context

Pure flat-rate SaaS pricing leaves money on the table when a customer scales usage 10× and Dealix captures none of the upside. Value-metric pricing aligns Dealix's revenue with the customer's outcomes — and gives the expansion playbook (T19) a built-in growth lever. This doc defines the value metrics that determine tier overages and natural expansion paths.

## 2. Audience

CRO, sales, CS. Used by quote-to-cash and consumed by expansion playbook (T19).

## 3. Decisions / Content

### 3.1 The three Saudi-relevant value metrics

1. **Enriched leads delivered per month** — primary engine output, easiest to attribute to revenue.
2. **Ranked-A leads consumed by actions** — true value moment (a touched A-lead has business outcome).
3. **Revenue-impact pipeline** (advanced, Enterprise+) — pipeline sourced from Dealix as % of total pipeline.

### 3.2 Why these three (not seats)

- **Seats** are vanity in B2B revenue tooling — companies share logins. Bad value metric.
- **API calls** are too engineering-centric — buyers don't think in API calls.
- **Leads + Ranked-A** maps directly to revenue. **Revenue-impact** is the gold standard but requires CRM integration for measurement.

### 3.3 Tier value-metric caps

| Tier | Enriched/mo | Ranked-A/mo | Revenue-pipeline-attributable |
|------|------------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Starter | 1,000 | 200 | n/a |
| Growth | 5,000 | 1,000 | tracked, not billed |
| Enterprise | 50,000 | 10,000 | optional revenue-share band |
| Sovereign | unlimited | unlimited | optional |

### 3.4 Overage pricing

- Starter overages: SAR 0.50 / enriched lead, SAR 2.50 / ranked-A lead. Auto-billed monthly.
- Growth overages: SAR 0.40 / enriched, SAR 2.00 / ranked-A.
- Enterprise: negotiated; typical SAR 0.25 / enriched, SAR 1.25 / ranked-A.

### 3.5 Revenue-share band (Enterprise add-on, optional)

For Enterprise customers willing to share revenue rather than pay flat:
- Customer attaches Dealix to a defined revenue motion (e.g., new BFSI business banking acquisition).
- Dealix gets 2–4% of net-new ARR sourced from Dealix-attributed pipeline (CRM integration verifies).
- Floor: SAR 400K/year (replaces flat).
- Ceiling: 2× flat-rate Enterprise (to protect customer in run-away scenarios).
- Measured monthly, reconciled quarterly.

### 3.6 Expansion natural levers (T19 hook)

When a customer hits 80% of tier cap in any of {enriched, ranked-A, seats}, CS auto-opens an expansion conversation per T19. When 100% hit twice in 60 days, an Enterprise upgrade quote is auto-generated by CS-OS.

### 3.7 Anti-shelfware safeguard

- If a customer uses < 30% of paid leads quota for 3 months: CS triggers a "value rescue" play (training, use-case alignment) instead of upsell. Renewals from "value rescue" accounts get +90 days bonus on renewal to rebuild trust.

## 4. KPIs

- NRR uplift from expansion driven by value metrics: ≥ 15% in 180 days.
- % of Enterprise customers on revenue-share band: 20% target by year-end 2.
- Anti-shelfware activation rate: > 60% of underutilized accounts saved.
