# Saudi / MENA Positioning

Saudi Arabia is building a national AI stack — infrastructure, data centers, cloud, and Arabic-first models. Dealix is **not** competing with that layer. Dealix sits **on top of it** and converts it into operating capability inside companies.

```
HUMAIN / infrastructure / cloud / models
Dealix / operations / workflows / governance / proof
```

## 1. Public-facing one-liners

- **EN:** Saudi Arabia is building AI infrastructure. Dealix helps companies operationalize AI safely on top of it.
- **AR:** السعودية تبني بنية الذكاء الاصطناعي. Dealix تساعد الشركات على تشغيل AI داخل أعمالها بأمان وقياس.

## 2. Why this framing wins

- It removes the false choice between “buy a national platform” and “buy a foreign AI tool.”
- It puts Dealix in a complementary, non-competitive position to national programs and platforms.
- It transfers Dealix’s credibility from “vendor” to “operator of national AI on the company floor.”
- It addresses the actual reported blockers in enterprise AI adoption — data security, privacy, and compliance — which surveys consistently rank as top concerns.

## 3. Where Dealix plugs in

| Layer | National stack | Dealix layer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Compute / cloud | National providers | Consumed via Core OS |
| Models | Arabic + multimodal | Routed via `llm_gateway` |
| Identity / data residency | National rules | Encoded in governance rule packs |
| Workflows | Per company | Built and governed by Dealix |
| Proof of value | Per company | Produced and owned by Dealix |

## 4. Market motion

- Use national AI events (e.g., LEAP, DeepFest) as **distribution moments**, not sales gimmicks.
- Lead with Capability Diagnostic offers, never with hype demos.
- Publish proof-backed Arabic content; Arabic is a strategic asset, not a translation afterthought.
- Build partner relationships with regulated industries first (financial services, healthcare, government-adjacent), because they value the runtime governance most.

## 5. Compliance posture

- Default to in-region data residency.
- Default to draft-only outputs unless governance explicitly allows external action.
- Default to Arabic governance disclosures alongside English.
- Document each engagement’s data handling in the Source Passport (`data_os`).

## 6. What Dealix avoids

- Public claims of being the national stack.
- Side-by-side comparisons with national initiatives.
- Building competing infrastructure.
- Marketing-language posturing without proof packs.

## 7. Anti-pattern: “Saudi AI agency”

Calling Dealix a “Saudi AI agency” underprices the firm and locks it into a labor business. The Endgame position is **Saudi/MENA Governed AI Operations Holding Company** — agency work is a wedge, not the company.
