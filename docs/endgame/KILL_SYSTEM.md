# Kill System

Power is not only in building. It is in stopping. The Kill System encodes the criteria and procedure for retiring services, features, markets, and ventures before they erode the firm.

## 1. Default bias

Kill is preferred to drift. A service quietly losing margin, a feature quietly accumulating maintenance debt, or a market quietly burning operator time is more dangerous than a clean termination.

## 2. Kill a Service when

- Win rate is low.
- Margin is low.
- Scope creep is high.
- Proof is weak.
- No retainer path exists.
- Governance risk is high.
- The work is not repeatable.

## 3. Kill a Feature when

- It is not reused across engagements.
- It is not tied to revenue, proof, governance, or operator efficiency.
- Maintenance cost exceeds delivery value.
- It does not reduce delivery effort.

## 4. Kill a Market when

- The buyer is unclear.
- Budget is consistently weak.
- The sales cycle is too long for the engagement size.
- Data risk is too high to satisfy governance.
- No proof path can be built within reasonable time.

## 5. Kill a Venture when

- It has missed its 12-month plan milestones.
- It is forking the Core OS instead of inheriting.
- It cannot show retainers within its first stage.
- It is consuming Dealix operator time without a payoff to the Capital Ledger.

## 6. Procedure

1. **Trigger** — Intelligence OS surfaces the candidate.
2. **Review** — the responsible BU lead and the Office of the Standard meet.
3. **Decision** — `KILL`, `PAUSE`, or `RECOMMIT WITH CRITERIA`.
4. **Communication** — internal and, when applicable, external messaging.
5. **Capital Sweep** — surviving assets are migrated to the Capital Ledger.
6. **Log** — the decision is recorded with inputs.

## 7. Communication discipline

- Internal: clear, written, and aimed at the team running the killed item.
- External: respectful, brief, focused on what continues to be supported.
- Never blame the team. The Kill System exists to protect operators from being trapped in dying work.

## 8. What never gets killed

- The Core OS.
- Governance Runtime.
- The Proof Pack format.
- The Capability Diagnostic as a paid entry.
- Capital Ledger discipline.
- The seven prime directives.

These are constitutional and persist across markets, services, and ventures.

## 9. Failure modes

- Refusing to kill because a team owner is loud.
- Killing without a Capital Sweep — losing the assets that the dead work produced.
- Letting `PAUSE` become “undecided forever.”
- Not communicating the kill, so the market keeps believing the offer still exists.

## 10. The principle

> What you refuse to kill, you are choosing to feed.
