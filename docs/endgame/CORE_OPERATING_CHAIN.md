# Core Operating Chain

The operating chain is the canonical sequence by which Dealix converts a signal in the market into compounding company capital. Each stage has explicit inputs, outputs, owners, and exit criteria. Skipping a stage is treated as a strategic violation by the Intelligence OS.

```
Signal
→ Capability Diagnostic
→ Productized Sprint
→ Governed Delivery
→ QA
→ Proof Pack
→ Retainer
→ Capital Asset
→ Product Module
→ Business Unit
→ Standard
→ Academy / Partner
→ Venture
→ Holding Company
```

## 1. Signal

- **Inputs:** inbound conversation, content engagement, partner referral, diagnostic request.
- **Owner:** Revenue.
- **Exit criteria:** intent + budget + pain identified; logged in the signal registry.

## 2. Capability Diagnostic

- **Inputs:** signal + 60-minute structured assessment.
- **Owner:** Revenue + Senior Operator.
- **Exit criteria:** Capability Score Report delivered (`CAPABILITY_DIAGNOSTIC.md`).

## 3. Productized Sprint

- **Inputs:** diagnostic recommendation.
- **Owner:** Delivery Lead of the relevant Business Unit.
- **Exit criteria:** signed SOW with fixed scope, fixed price, fixed proof.

## 4. Governed Delivery

- **Inputs:** sprint kickoff + access + data pass.
- **Owner:** Delivery + Governance Runtime.
- **Exit criteria:** every output runs through `governance_os`; audit log complete.

## 5. QA

- **Inputs:** delivery artifacts.
- **Owner:** independent QA reviewer (not the operator who built it).
- **Exit criteria:** QA rubric passed at ≥ 80%.

## 6. Proof Pack

- **Inputs:** delivery artifacts + governance events + metrics.
- **Owner:** Reporting OS.
- **Exit criteria:** signed Proof Pack — see `PROOF_ECONOMY.md`.

## 7. Retainer

- **Inputs:** Proof Pack + retainer offer.
- **Owner:** Revenue.
- **Exit criteria:** monthly contract signed *or* explicit pause/decline logged.

## 8. Capital Asset

- **Inputs:** sprint artifacts.
- **Owner:** Capital OS.
- **Exit criteria:** at least one asset entered into the Capital Ledger — see `CAPITAL_LEDGER_STRATEGY.md`.

## 9. Product Module

- **Inputs:** repeated capital assets across ≥ 3 engagements.
- **Owner:** Product.
- **Exit criteria:** Core OS module spec + first internal implementation.

## 10. Business Unit

- **Inputs:** repeatable offer + delivery playbook + ≥ 5 paid engagements.
- **Owner:** BU Lead.
- **Exit criteria:** BU charter (see `BUSINESS_UNITS.md`).

## 11. Standard

- **Inputs:** maturity in delivery, governance, proof.
- **Owner:** Office of the Standard.
- **Exit criteria:** Dealix Method documented + QA rubric + benchmark.

## 12. Academy / Partner

- **Inputs:** standard + 10+ engagements + 3+ proof-backed case assets.
- **Owner:** Academy / Partner Office.
- **Exit criteria:** certification track open — see `ACADEMY_STRATEGY.md`.

## 13. Venture

- **Inputs:** product module + retainers + standalone economics — see `VENTURE_FACTORY.md`.
- **Owner:** Venture Council.
- **Exit criteria:** venture entity scoped, owner appointed, kill criteria set.

## 14. Holding Company

- **Inputs:** multiple business units and ventures with shared Core OS dependency.
- **Owner:** Dealix Group.
- **Exit criteria:** capital allocation, cross-BU governance, standards stewardship.

## Forbidden jumps

- Academy **without** a proven method.
- Partner program **without** QA.
- Venture **without** retainers.
- SaaS **without** client pull.
- Enterprise **without** a trust pack.

## Health metric

The chain has one operating metric: **stage-to-stage conversion**. If the Diagnostic → Sprint conversion drops, the wedge is wrong. If Sprint → Retainer drops, delivery or proof is weak. If Retainer → Capital Asset drops, the firm is doing service, not building a company.
