# Business Units

Business units are formal lines of operating capability inside Dealix. A BU exists only when it has a repeatable offer, a delivery playbook, paid demand, and a named leader.

## 1. Initial BU map

| BU | Primary Offer | Recurring Offer | Product Module | Proof Type |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dealix Revenue | Revenue Intelligence Sprint | Monthly RevOps OS | Revenue OS | Revenue Proof Pack |
| Dealix Brain | Company Brain Sprint | Monthly Company Brain | Knowledge OS | Knowledge Proof Pack |
| Dealix Governance | AI Governance Review | Monthly Governance | Governance Runtime | Risk Proof Pack |
| Dealix Operations | AI Quick Win Sprint | Monthly AI Ops | Workflow OS | Time/Quality Proof Pack |

## 2. Dealix Revenue

- **KPIs:** accounts scored, pipeline value, draft packs approved, retainer conversion.
- **Operating cadence:** weekly pipeline review, monthly proof refresh.
- **Risk surface:** outbound governance, data residency.
- **Expansion path:** revenue → governance → operations.

## 3. Dealix Brain

- **KPIs:** docs indexed, citation coverage, insufficient-evidence rate, knowledge gaps closed.
- **Operating cadence:** weekly source refresh, monthly knowledge eval.
- **Risk surface:** confidentiality, retrieval drift.
- **Expansion path:** brain → revenue (sales enablement) and brain → operations (workflow-aware answers).

## 4. Dealix Governance

- **KPIs:** rules created, risky actions blocked, approvals logged, audit coverage.
- **Operating cadence:** weekly rule tuning, monthly executive audit report.
- **Risk surface:** false negatives, runtime gaps, audit completeness.
- **Expansion path:** governance becomes the backbone for every other BU.

## 5. Dealix Operations

- **KPIs:** hours saved, workflows created, approvals completed, error reduction.
- **Operating cadence:** weekly workflow review, monthly time-saving proof.
- **Risk surface:** brittle automations, dependency on third-party tools.
- **Expansion path:** operations → brain (workflow-aware knowledge) → revenue (lead-handling workflows).

## 6. Charter requirements

A BU charter must include:

- Named leader, second-in-command, and QA reviewer.
- Repeatable offer set tied to the productized stack.
- Delivery playbook with QA rubric.
- Proof template aligned with the BU’s proof type.
- Capital Ledger ownership map.
- 12-month operating plan.

## 7. Promotion criteria

A capability becomes a BU when **all** are true:

- 5+ paid engagements completed.
- 2+ active retainers.
- Repeatable delivery (QA rubric passes ≥ 80%).
- Clear product module dependency.
- Healthy gross margin.
- Named owner ready.
- Proof library has at least 3 strong assets.

## 8. Cross-BU rules

- All BUs share `core_os`, `governance_os`, `reporting_os`, `intelligence_os`, `capital_os`.
- No BU forks the governance runtime.
- No BU runs an offer that bypasses the productized stack.
- Cross-BU work uses the same Proof Pack format.

## 9. Failure modes

- A BU forms before it has retainers — it then chases revenue and burns governance discipline.
- A BU forms around a single hero operator — it cannot scale.
- A BU runs custom work to hit numbers, breaking the productized model.
- BUs compete for the same buyer with different stories.
