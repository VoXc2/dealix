# Market Power

Market power is not measured in followers or impressions. It is measured by **how the market behaves around your category language and your standard**. The following indicators are the only ones Dealix tracks at the market level.

## 1. Indicators

- **Category Language Adoption** — buyers using Dealix terms (Governed AI Operations, Proof Pack, Capability Score) in their own RFPs and posts.
- **Inbound Diagnostic Requests** — paid Capability Diagnostic bookings without outbound prompting.
- **Partner Referrals** — partners sending qualified inbound.
- **Proof Pack Requests** — buyers asking for proof samples by name.
- **Benchmark Downloads** — sector benchmarks published by Dealix being downloaded and cited.
- **Academy Waitlist** — interest in tracks before sale.
- **Enterprise Trust Inquiries** — regulated buyers asking about runtime governance and audit.
- **Content-to-Call Conversion** — content that converts into a Diagnostic call.
- **Competitor Copy Signals** — competitors adopting Dealix terms in their materials.

## 2. Interpretation rules

- **Proof Pack requests rising** → the trust mechanism has become a product.
- **Capability Score requests rising** → the category language is spreading.
- **Partner certifications requested** → distribution is forming.
- **Competitor copy** → the category is being shaped.

When at least three of the above are sustained for two quarters, Dealix should escalate market spend — not before.

## 3. Anti-vanity rules

- Do **not** track follower counts, likes, or impressions as primary metrics.
- Do **not** chase awards that are not tied to proof.
- Do **not** publish thought leadership without a proof anchor.
- Do **not** participate in panels that conflict with refused buyers.

## 4. Channels

- **Founder voice** — long-form, proof-anchored.
- **Arabic-first content** — strategic, not translated.
- **Benchmarks** — quarterly sector data based on Proof Library.
- **Briefings** — small-group sessions with senior buyers.
- **Stage** — national events (LEAP/DeepFest) used as **distribution moments**.

## 5. Content discipline

- Every claim sources to a Proof Pack ID.
- Every benchmark sources to a data set the firm owns or licenses.
- Every case asset is gated by consent and evidence level.
- Every Arabic piece has an Arabic-native voice review.

## 6. Cadence

- Monthly: founder essay tied to one proof.
- Quarterly: sector benchmark report.
- Quarterly: executive briefing series.
- Annually: State of Governed AI Operations report.

## 7. Failure modes

- Treating content as media instead of as a sales motion.
- Publishing claims that exceed the underlying evidence level.
- Optimizing for reach over Capability Diagnostic conversion.
- Sponsoring events without measuring inbound impact.

## 8. The endgame

Dealix wins market power when **buyers, partners, and regulators** all use the same words Dealix uses, and reach for Dealix when they need an AI capability to be operable, governed, and provable.
