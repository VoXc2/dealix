## Context

Dealix is not a generic agency. It is a factory that turns business
problems into AI-enabled operating capabilities. This file defines the
factory itself — its purpose, its flow, and the rule that every service
must improve a named client capability. It plugs Layer 4 into the wider
operating model defined in `docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`
and the safety boundary set by `docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md`.
Without this layer, services become disconnected deliverables; with it,
every engagement compounds into client capability and Dealix proof.

## Purpose

The AI Capability Factory exists to:

- Translate raw client problems into named, measurable capabilities.
- Standardize how every engagement is intaken, built, governed, and
  delivered with proof.
- Force every service in `docs/COMPANY_SERVICE_LADDER.md` to land on
  one of seven capabilities (Revenue, Customer, Operations, Knowledge,
  Data, Governance, Reporting).
- Create a recurring expansion path from sprint → pilot → retainer.

## Factory Flow

Every engagement moves through eleven stages, in order:

1. **Intake** — capture client problem, context, and goal.
2. **Capability Assessment** — name the capability and current level.
3. **Data Readiness** — score sources, governance, and PII handling.
4. **Workflow Design** — map the 8-step workflow (see
   `docs/product/WORKFLOW_RUNTIME_DESIGN.md`).
5. **Governance Design** — wire approvals, audit, and risk controls.
6. **AI Build** — assemble agents, prompts, tools, and runtime.
7. **QA + Evals** — pass eval gates before any client output.
8. **Delivery** — operate the workflow on client data.
9. **Proof Pack** — generate signed proof event(s).
10. **Capability Roadmap** — next level, next workflow, next service.
11. **Retainer / Expansion** — convert to recurring operating model.

## Capability Rule

Rule: every service must improve at least one client capability —
Revenue, Customer, Operations, Knowledge, Data, Governance, or
Reporting. Services that do not name a capability cannot enter the
ladder. This rule is enforced at intake and at QA.

## Sales Positioning

Instead of "we have many services," Dealix says: "we identify which
capability you need to build first, then choose the right service."
This positioning is the operational backbone of the category narrative
in `docs/strategic/DEALIX_CATEGORY_EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY_EN.md`.

## Metrics

- Capability assignment rate — % of engagements with a named capability at intake.
- Time to first proof — days from intake to first proof event.
- Sprint-to-retainer conversion — % of sprints that expand to retainer.
- Capability level improvement — average level lift per client.
- Factory throughput — engagements completed per month.
