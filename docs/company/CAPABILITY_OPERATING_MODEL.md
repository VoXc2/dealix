## Context
Dealix does not sell projects in isolation; we build repeatable AI-enabled
business capabilities inside our clients. This file defines the seven
capabilities that all Dealix services compose against, and it draws the
line between a Service (a fixed-scope deliverable) and a Capability (an
ongoing client ability). This separation is required by
`docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md` and by the operating
constitution at `docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md`, which both
demand that every paid engagement leaves the client with a measurable
capability uplift, not just a one-off artefact.

## Core Capabilities
Dealix builds seven AI-enabled business capabilities. Each capability is
the unit that is assessed, levelled, sold, delivered, and reported on.

1. **Revenue Capability** — identifying, ranking, and converting
   opportunities into pipeline.
2. **Customer Capability** — understanding customers and replying with
   quality and speed.
3. **Operations Capability** — automating repetitive operations and
   reducing errors.
4. **Knowledge Capability** — turning scattered knowledge into
   source-cited answers.
5. **Data Capability** — preparing data for decisions and AI.
6. **Governance Capability** — using AI safely with approvals and logs.
7. **Reporting Capability** — letting management see truth and decide.

Every Dealix service map back to one or more capabilities, every
Capability has a level (see `CAPABILITY_MATURITY_MODEL.md`), and every
Sprint or Pilot must declare which capability it raises and by how many
levels.

## Service vs Capability
A Service is a time-bound engagement; a Capability is the ability that
service leaves behind.

| Service (deliverable) | Capability (durable ability) |
|---|---|
| Lead Intelligence Sprint — 10-day project | Revenue Capability — ongoing ability to rank opportunities |
| Company Brain Sprint — build an assistant | Knowledge Capability — internal ability to access knowledge |
| AI Quick Win — one automation | Operations Capability — continuous improvement of operations |
| Governance Review — risk report | Governance Capability — ongoing governance ability |

Rule: every Sprint starts capability-building, not just deliverable
shipping. The Statement of Work, the Proof Pack, and the Customer
Success handoff must each name the capability uplift in measurable
terms.

## Operating principles
- **Capability before tool.** Tools are only purchased once a capability
  level has been agreed for the next quarter.
- **Capability before deliverable.** A deliverable that does not raise a
  capability is treated as waste.
- **Capability before headcount.** New hires are sized to the capability
  portfolio, not the project pipeline.
- **Capability before content.** Marketing and category narrative are
  written around the seven capabilities, not around features.

## Interfaces
| Inputs | Outputs | Owners | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client request, Discovery notes, Existing data sources | Capability scores (7), Recommended Sprint, 90-day capability roadmap | Founder, Delivery Lead | Per engagement |
| Quarterly capability portfolio review | Sprint demand forecast, Hiring plan, Training plan | Founder | Quarterly |
| Sprint and Pilot post-mortems | Updated capability playbooks, New eval cases | Delivery Lead, QA Lead | Per delivery |

## Metrics
- **Capability Uplift per Engagement** — average level delta across the
  seven capabilities per paid Sprint (target ≥ +1.5 levels on the
  primary capability).
- **Capability Conversion Rate** — share of Sprints where the client
  buys the next capability stage within 60 days (target ≥ 50%).
- **Capability Coverage** — number of the seven capabilities Dealix can
  deliver at L4 quality (target 7/7 by end of Year 1).
- **Capability Re-engagement Revenue** — revenue from clients returning
  to raise the same or another capability (target ≥ 40% of MRR by month
  12).
