# Capability Factory Map

> Standalone, citable table used in proposals, sales calls, and
> partner enablement. Companion to `AI_CAPABILITY_FACTORY.md`. Each row
> shows how one input problem becomes an operating capability, which
> Dealix service builds it, what proof customers receive, and where the
> account naturally expands.

## The 7-row map

| # | Input Problem (customer voice) | Capability Built | Dealix Service | Proof Type | Expansion |
|---|--------------------------------|------------------|----------------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | "We have 2,000 leads in a spreadsheet and no idea which to call." | Revenue Capability | Lead Intelligence Sprint | Before/after Account Quality Snapshot + ranked top-50 + 10 next actions | Pilot Conversion Sprint → Monthly RevOps OS |
| 2 | "My team spends 6 hours a week on the same manual report." | Operations Capability | AI Quick Win Sprint | Time-study before/after + runbook + audit log | Workflow Automation Sprint → Monthly AI Ops |
| 3 | "Nobody can find the right SOP / policy / contract clause." | Knowledge Capability | Company Brain Sprint | Cited-answer eval (≥95% citation) + sample Q&A pack | Policy Assistant / Sales Knowledge Assistant → Enterprise Brain |
| 4 | "We answer the same 50 support questions every week." | Customer Capability | AI Support Desk Sprint *(Phase 2)* | First-response-time delta + suggested-reply eval | Monthly Support AI retainer |
| 5 | "Our CRM is full but unusable; data isn't trustworthy for AI." | Data Capability | Data Cleanup Sprint | Data Readiness Score v1 vs v2 + PDPL register snippet | Monthly RevOps / Monthly AI Ops |
| 6 | "We're scared to use AI because of compliance and audit." | Governance Capability | AI Governance Program *(Phase 3)* | Governance dashboard + audit export + approval-matrix coverage | Enterprise AI OS path |
| 7 | "Leadership can't see what happened this week in time to act." | Reporting Capability | Executive Reporting Automation *(Phase 2)* | Weekly executive pack + decision log + time-to-report delta | Reporting retainer → multi-capability OS |

## How to use this map

- **Sales call**: prospect describes a problem in their words → rep
  points at the row → frame moves from "tool" to "capability lift".
- **Proposal**: the row's Service column drives Tier 2 pricing; the
  Proof Type is what the SOW commits to deliver at Stage 7.
- **Partner enablement**: this is the one-page Dealix value model;
  partners can resell on the language in this table.
- **Roadmapping**: the Expansion column drives 60-day post-sprint
  outreach (per `CLIENT_JOURNEY.md`).

## Why 7 rows, not more

The 7 capabilities (per `CAPABILITY_OPERATING_MODEL.md`) form the
operating spine of any Saudi B2B company. Adding more rows fragments
the message; collapsing rows loses the upsell path. 7 is the binding
count until the operating model itself changes.

## Rule

If a prospect's problem doesn't map to a row above, the answer is
**Diagnostic first** (Tier 1, `IMPLEMENTATION_TIERS.md`). Never quote
a Sprint against an unmapped problem — capability assessment must
precede capability building.

## Saudi context

Rows 5 and 6 (Data + Governance) are the most common procurement
gating items in Saudi enterprises post-PDPL enforcement. Selling Rows
1–4 without anchoring Rows 5–6 leads to deals that stall in legal
review.
