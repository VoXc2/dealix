# Capability Value Map

> Standalone reference for the Value Realization layer
> (`VALUE_REALIZATION_SYSTEM.md`). One line per capability connecting
> the **business value statement** to the **services that build it**
> and the **proof type** customers receive. Use it in every pitch:
> customers do not buy services — they buy capability lifts.

## The map

| Capability | Business Value (customer voice) | Services that build it | Primary Proof Type |
|------------|---------------------------------|------------------------|--------------------|
| Revenue Capability | "We see and act on revenue we were missing." | Lead Intelligence Sprint · Pilot Conversion · Monthly RevOps OS | Ranked top-50 accounts + before/after Account Quality Snapshot |
| Customer Capability | "Our customers get faster, more consistent answers." | AI Support Desk Sprint *(Phase 2)* · Monthly Customer Support AI | First-response-time delta + suggested-reply eval |
| Operations Capability | "Our team stops doing the same manual thing every week." | AI Quick Win Sprint · Workflow Automation Sprint · Monthly AI Ops | Time-study before/after + audit log + runbook |
| Knowledge Capability | "Anyone in our company can find the right answer with a source." | Company Brain Sprint · Policy Assistant · Sales Knowledge Assistant | Cited-answer eval (≥ 95%) + sample Q&A pack |
| Data Capability | "Our data is trustworthy enough to make decisions and run AI on." | Data Cleanup Sprint · Monthly RevOps (data hygiene) | Data Readiness Score v1 vs v2 + PDPL register snippet |
| Governance Capability | "We use AI safely — every action is approved, logged, and PDPL-compliant." | AI Governance Program *(Phase 3)* | Governance dashboard + audit export + approval-matrix coverage |
| Reporting Capability | "Leadership sees the truth in time to act on Monday morning." | Executive Reporting Automation · Monthly Reporting retainer | Weekly executive pack + decision log + report-to-action time |

## Sales reframe (verbatim use)

| Old framing (industry default) | Dealix capability framing |
|--------------------------------|---------------------------|
| "We are selling you a Company Brain Sprint." | "We are taking your Knowledge Capability from Level 1 to Level 3 in 21 days." |
| "Our AI tool automates this." | "We build the Operations Capability that operates this safely every week." |
| "Buy a chatbot." | "Buy a 90-day path from manual support to Governed Customer Capability." |
| "Here's our proposal." | "Here's your capability roadmap — Sprint, Pilot, Retainer." |

## Why this matters commercially

- **Pricing power**: capability lifts justify SAR 9,500–60,000 / mo
  retainers; deliverables do not.
- **Retention**: customers can describe what they got — a capability
  level moved — and renew.
- **Upsell**: the next capability is the next sale; the map is the
  literal expansion menu.
- **Differentiation**: this map is the antidote to the "AI agency"
  category (`CATEGORY_DESIGN.md`).

## The binding rule

A proposal that does **not** name the capability being lifted and the
level delta targeted is **not a Dealix proposal**. CRO rejects it
before it reaches the customer.

## Saudi context

The Business Value column is the language Saudi executives use in
their own board meetings. Drafting proposals in this language (AR + EN)
shortens sales cycles measurably; per
`docs/strategy/dealix_operating_partner_positioning.md`, the
capability-first framing is also the bilingual default.
