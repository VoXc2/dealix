## Context
Dealix services are easy to misread as point tools ("we build a Company
Brain"). Buyers reach for capabilities, not tools. This file maps Dealix
capabilities to the business value they unlock, the services that produce
that value, and the primary proof category each tends to generate. It is
the bridge between `docs/COMPANY_SERVICE_LADDER.md`,
`docs/OFFER_LADDER_AND_PRICING.md`, and `docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`.
With it, every sales conversation can be reframed from "tool delivered" to
"capability raised — and here is the proof."

## The map

| Capability | Business Value | Services | Primary Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | more pipeline clarity & faster follow-up | Lead Intelligence, RevOps OS | Revenue |
| Customer | faster & consistent support | Support Desk | Time / Quality |
| Operations | fewer manual steps & errors | Quick Win, Workflow Automation | Time / Quality |
| Knowledge | faster source-backed answers | Company Brain | Knowledge |
| Data | cleaner inputs for AI & decisions | Data Readiness | Quality / Risk |
| Governance | safer AI usage | AI Governance | Risk |
| Reporting | better executive decisions | Executive Reporting | Quality / Time |

## Sales reframe

Instead of "we build a Company Brain," the conversation becomes:

> "We raise your **Knowledge Capability** from scattered files to
> source-cited answers — which reduces search time and increases
> consistency."

Apply this pattern to every capability:

- **Revenue Capability** — from "more outbound" to "ranked accounts, scored
  open opportunities, surfaced follow-ups."
- **Customer Capability** — from "more agents" to "faster, more consistent
  replies with safer escalation."
- **Operations Capability** — from "automate it" to "fewer manual steps and
  fewer errors with audit logs."
- **Data Capability** — from "data cleanup" to "clean inputs that make AI
  decisions reliable."
- **Governance Capability** — from "AI policy" to "executable rules and
  approvals that block unsafe actions."
- **Reporting Capability** — from "pretty dashboards" to "decisions
  executives can act on this week."

## How a buyer enters the map

1. Buyer states pain — "we waste hours on lead enrichment."
2. We name the capability — "this is an Operations + Revenue gap."
3. We pick a starting service — Quick Win or Lead Intelligence.
4. We define the proof category up front — Time + Revenue.
5. We propose the next-value step — Monthly AI Ops or Pilot Conversion.

## Internal use

- Sales sequences cite capabilities, not tools.
- Proof packs declare capability raised, not just deliverables shipped.
- Pricing tiers correspond to capability depth — not feature count.
- Hiring and roadmap decisions follow the strongest capability signals
  from `docs/ledgers/VALUE_LEDGER.md`.

## Metrics
- Capability Coverage — % of services mapped to a capability and a proof category.
- Capability Strength — ledger entries per capability over trailing 90 days.
- Capability Pricing Power — average margin by capability.
- Reframe Adoption — % of pitch materials using capability language.
