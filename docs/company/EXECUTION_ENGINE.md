## Context
The Execution Engine is the weekly machine that turns Dealix strategy into
shipped work. It removes the most expensive failure mode in a founder-led
AI services company — long stretches where one motion (build, sell, or
deliver) starves the others. By forcing all six loops to advance every
week, this file aligns the company with the operating cadence defined in
`docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md` and the discipline encoded in
`docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md`. It is the runtime under L7 of
the Dealix operating layers.

## The Six Loops

Dealix operates through six execution loops. Each loop has a single
question, a single output, and a single owner default.

1. **Market Loop** — find painful business problems in a focused vertical.
   - Question: *who has the most expensive version of this pain right now?*
   - Output: 20 target accounts named, classified, prioritized.
2. **Sales Loop** — convert pain into a paid diagnostic or sprint.
   - Question: *what would make this account pay this month?*
   - Output: 10 manual, personalized messages or conversations.
3. **Delivery Loop** — deliver measurable output with QA and governance.
   - Question: *what does the client need this week to feel value?*
   - Output: 1 delivery milestone shipped or 1 working demo.
4. **Proof Loop** — turn delivery into proof, case assets, value metrics.
   - Question: *what evidence did we just earn the right to publish?*
   - Output: 1 sample report, anonymized proof pack, or value metric.
5. **Product Loop** — turn repeated manual work into product modules.
   - Question: *what did we do twice manually this month?*
   - Output: 1 module built, refined, or scoped for next sprint.
6. **Retainer Loop** — turn proven value into monthly operating cadence.
   - Question: *which delivery can become a monthly?*
   - Output: 1 continuation offer made.

## The Rule

> Every week must move at least one item in each of the six loops.

If any loop is at zero on Friday, the week did not happen. This is the
non-negotiable cadence. It is more important than any single deal,
feature, or fire.

## Ideal Week (founder-solo baseline)

| Loop | Weekly minimum |
|---|---|
| Market | 20 target accounts touched in CRM |
| Sales | 10 manual personalized messages |
| Delivery | 1 delivery milestone or demo |
| Proof | 1 proof artifact (report, pack, metric) |
| Product | 1 module built or improved |
| Retainer | 1 continuation / renewal conversation |

When the founder is alone, these numbers are the floor. When a CSM or
delivery hire joins (see `docs/HIRING_CSM_FIRST.md`), Delivery and
Retainer minimums double before Market and Sales.

## How loops feed each other

```
Market  ──► Sales   ──► Delivery ──► Proof
   ▲                                    │
   │                                    ▼
Retainer ◄── Product ◄────────────── Proof
```

Proof is the pivot. Without proof there is no Product backlog and no
Retainer expansion. This is why Proof Loop discipline is mandatory even
when delivery is hot.

## Loop owners (today and at first hire)

| Loop | Today (solo) | After CSM hire | After delivery hire |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market | Founder | Founder | Founder |
| Sales | Founder | Founder | Founder |
| Delivery | Founder | CSM + Founder | Delivery Lead |
| Proof | Founder | CSM | CSM |
| Product | Founder | Founder | Founder + contractor |
| Retainer | Founder | CSM | CSM |

## Anti-patterns

- A week of building with zero outbound — Market and Sales went to zero.
- A week of selling with zero delivery — clients lose trust, proof dies.
- A delivery week with no proof asset — no upsell, no case study fuel.
- A product sprint not tied to repetition observed in delivery — premature.

## Interfaces
| Inputs | Outputs | Owners | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pipeline list, CRM, vertical brief | 20 named accounts | Founder | Weekly |
| Account list, ICP brief | 10 personalized messages | Founder | Weekly |
| Signed SOW, intake form | 1 milestone or demo | Founder / Delivery Lead | Weekly |
| Delivery artifact | Proof pack / sample report | Founder / CSM | Weekly |
| Manual delivery patterns | Module or workflow upgrade | Founder | Weekly |
| Active client roster | 1 continuation offer | Founder / CSM | Weekly |

## Metrics
- Loop completion rate — % of weeks where all six loops moved (target 100%).
- Proof per delivery — proof artifacts produced per delivery (target ≥ 1).
- Manual-to-module ratio — repeated tasks converted to modules per quarter.
- Continuation rate — % of completed sprints that produced a retainer offer.
