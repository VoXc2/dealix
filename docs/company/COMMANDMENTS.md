## Context

Ten rules. They cap the entire operating manual. When in doubt — at
proposal, in delivery, in hiring, in marketing — return here first.
They are the constitutional shortcuts referenced from
`docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md` and from
`docs/company/DEALIX_CEO_STRATEGY.md`.

## The Ten

1. **Sell outcomes, not tools.** Buyers pay for changed metrics, not
   for AI features.
2. **Start narrow, compound deep.** Three offers, three verticals,
   three months — then expand from proof.
3. **No proof, no claim.** Every public claim is backed by a Proof
   Pack, anonymized metric, or approved testimonial.
4. **No source, no answer.** Every AI-generated answer cites its
   source data or it is not delivered.
5. **No approval, no external action.** No automated message,
   purchase, contract, or external write happens without human
   approval.
6. **No governance, no enterprise.** We do not sell to regulated
   buyers until governance, PDPL, audit, and approvals are wired.
7. **No repeated demand, no feature.** We do not build a feature for
   one client; we build after three asks across two segments.
8. **No margin, no scale.** A track below its margin floor does not
   grow until the floor is restored.
9. **No Arabic quality, no Saudi trust.** Arabic Business Quality
   score ≥85 is non-negotiable on client-visible output.
10. **No capital created, no strategic project.** Every strategic
    project deposits Service, Product, Knowledge, Trust, or Market
    capital — or it is not strategic.
