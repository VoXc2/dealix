## Context
Numbers tell what happened; narrative tells what it meant. The Monthly Executive Narrative is a short, plain-language note from the CEO that travels with the Board Pack and the Enterprise AI Report Card. It frames the month's data into the company's strategic direction. It draws on `docs/EXECUTIVE_DECISION_PACK.md`, the founder's operating rhythm in `docs/V14_FOUNDER_DAILY_OPS.md`, and the KPI spec in `docs/BUSINESS_KPI_DASHBOARD_SPEC.md`.

## Structure
The narrative is six paragraphs, each tightly scoped. The order is fixed; the content is fresh every month.

### 1. What improved
What got better this month — specific, measurable, attributable. Naming the team or person responsible is encouraged.

### 2. What broke
What did not work — failed engagements, missed targets, incidents. Honest, specific, attributable. If the cause is unknown, that is stated too.

### 3. What we learned
The insight from improvement and breakage combined — what we now believe about our customers, our market, our agents, or our process that we didn't believe a month ago.

### 4. What we will stop doing
The explicit list of things Dealix will stop doing next month — bad-fit offers, low-margin work, ineffective rituals, vanity metrics. Stopping is a decision, and decisions go in the narrative.

### 5. What we will double down on
The explicit list of things Dealix will invest more in next month — winning offers, growing segments, working agents, partner channels, governance maturity.

### 6. What this means strategically
The connection back to the strategic plan. Are we on the V14 trajectory, ahead, or behind? Is the category we are building still the right one? Is the customer evidence consistent with our thesis? If not, what is the proposed shift?

## Length
- One page max.
- ~600–800 words.
- Plain language; no internal jargon; no acronyms without expansion.
- No charts (charts live in the Board Pack and the Report Card).

## Distribution
- Sent with the Board Pack to the board.
- Shared with the leadership team.
- Excerpts shared with the broader Dealix team in the monthly all-hands.
- Selected, anonymized lessons shared with enterprise clients where useful.

| Board Pack, Report Card, founder ops log | Six-paragraph narrative | CEO | Monthly |

| Strategic plan | "What this means strategically" anchor | CEO | Monthly |

## Metrics
- Narrative On-Time Rate — % of months with the narrative delivered before the board meeting.
- Decision-Following Rate — % of "stop" and "double down" items reflected in next month's plan.
- Learning-Logged Rate — % of monthly learnings captured in the strategic backlog.
- Reading Time — measured time the board spends on the document (should be < 6 minutes).
