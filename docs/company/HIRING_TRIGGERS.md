## Context
Premature hiring kills services firms. Dealix hires only when a
specific operational signal makes a role financially and operationally
inevitable. This file declares those signals. It works with
`docs/HIRING_CSM_FIRST.md` (which defines the first role) and
`docs/90_DAY_BUSINESS_EXECUTION_PLAN.md` (which time-frames hiring),
and inherits its overall stance from
`docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`.

## The four triggers

### First Delivery Analyst
Hire when all of:
- 3+ active projects in steady state.
- Delivery tasks repeat across projects.
- Templates and checklists are ready for someone to follow.
- Current and projected margin supports the seat.

### First Product Engineer
Hire when all of:
- 3+ repeated manual workflows identified.
- Internal tools materially save time when applied.
- Product backlog score > 80 (priority × reuse × time-saved).

### First Customer Success Manager
Hire when all of:
- 3+ retainers in book of business.
- Monthly reporting cycle repeats predictably.
- Client follow-up has become a bottleneck on founder time.

This trigger is also the subject of
`docs/HIRING_CSM_FIRST.md`, which expands the rationale.

### First Governance Reviewer
Hire when all of:
- Sensitive-data projects increase in frequency.
- Enterprise interest appears (formal RFPs, audits, security
  reviews).
- Compliance review has become a bottleneck on the founder.

## The system-first rule

> Hire only when a system exists for the person to follow.

A hire is a multiplier on an existing system. Hiring before the system
exists turns the new person into a second founder by accident, which
breaks the design of the firm and dilutes the role of the actual
founder. If a role's system is not yet defined, the hire is deferred
and the gap is logged as a build task in the operating loop.

## Anti-triggers

These conditions do **not** justify hiring even if money is available:

- A single client wants more done.
- A burst of demand from one quarter.
- A founder wishes for less work.
- A potential hire is good and wants to join.

Each of these creates pressure but none of them is a system signal.

## Workflow

1. The founder reviews the four triggers monthly.
2. When all sub-conditions of a trigger are true, the hiring brief is
   opened and the role is added to the operating roadmap.
3. The brief must specify the system the hire will inherit, the
   capital they are expected to compound, and the budget envelope.

## Metrics
- Time-from-trigger-to-hire — months between trigger firing and hire start; target ≤ 3.
- System-readiness score — share of hire briefs with a documented system; target 100%.
- Premature hires — count of hires made before all sub-conditions held; target 0.
- Post-hire margin impact — change in gross margin after hire, six months out; target ≥ ±0 points.
