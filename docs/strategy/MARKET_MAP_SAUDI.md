## Why now — five pressures

1. **Vision 2030 inflection.** PIF portfolio companies, NTP-linked
   programs, Saudi-Made / Local Content rules push enterprises toward
   Saudi-anchored vendors with audit trails.
2. **PDPL enforcement is real.** SDAIA issued 48 sanction decisions
   in 2025; fines up to SAR 5,000,000 per breach (doubled for repeat),
   up to 2 years prison for sensitive-data violations. Foreign "ship
   and pray" tools are now procurement-blocked.
3. **In-Kingdom residency is procurement-mandatory.** 70%+ of Saudi
   cloud SaaS on local DCs. Sovereign customers require single-tenant +
   KMS-BYOK.
4. **ZATCA Phase 2 e-invoicing.** Mandatory. Foreign tools without
   ZATCA integration cannot serve enterprise without a partner.
5. **AI buyer shift.** 48% of B2B buyers research vendors via AI tools
   (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) before the website. AI-referred
   sessions grew 527% YoY in early 2025. Saudi-native, bilingual,
   structurally-honest content gets cited within 1–12 weeks.

## Competitive classes

1. **US-translated stack** (SF + Outreach + Apollo + ZoomInfo) — no
   Saudi-native data, no Decision Passport, no PDPL.
2. **Local incumbent CRMs** — CRM-only, no engine, no AI scoring.
3. **Lead-list / agency** — per-list cost, no audit, breaks under PDPL.
4. **Status quo** (manual / spreadsheets) — does not scale, BDR burnout.

Dealix counter-positioning in `docs/strategy/competitive_landscape_sa.md`.

## Addressable opportunity (Dealix bottom-up)

- **Tier 1** (~800 named accounts): SAR 500K–3M ARR each → SAR 0.4–2.4B TAM.
- **Tier 2** (~6,000 firms, SAR 30M–200M revenue): SAR 100K–500K ARR →
  SAR 600M–3B TAM.
- **Tier 3** (700K+ VAT-registered B2B SMEs): SAR 24K–100K ARR each —
  large but PLG/long-tail.
