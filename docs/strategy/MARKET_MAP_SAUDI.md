## Why now — five structural pressures

1. **Vision 2030 inflection.** PIF portfolio companies, NTP-linked
   programs, and Saudi-Made / Local Content rules push enterprises
   toward Saudi-anchored vendors with audit trails.
2. **PDPL enforcement is real.** SDAIA issued 48 sanction decisions in
   2025; fines up to SAR 5,000,000 per breach (doubled for repeat),
   and up to 2 years prison for sensitive-data violations. Marketing
   without explicit opt-in is the most-punished category. Foreign tools
   that "ship and pray" are now procurement-blocked.
3. **In-Kingdom residency is procurement-mandatory.** 70%+ of Saudi
   cloud SaaS now sits on local DCs. Sovereign customers (govt, SAMA-
   regulated banks) require single-tenant + KMS-BYOK.
4. **ZATCA Phase 2 e-invoicing.** Mandatory. Foreign tools without
   ZATCA integration cannot serve enterprise customers without an
   integration partner — opening a wedge for Saudi-native vendors.
5. **AI buyer behavior shift.** 48% of B2B buyers now research vendors
   via AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) before visiting a website.
   AI-referred sessions grew 527% YoY in early 2025. Saudi-native,
   bilingual, structurally-honest content (e.g., Dealix's Service
   Activation Console) gets cited within 1–12 weeks.

## Competitive positioning (4 named classes)

1. **US-translated stack** (Salesforce + Outreach + Apollo + ZoomInfo) —
   no Saudi-native data, weak Arabic entity resolution, no Decision
   Passport, no PDPL enforcement.
2. **Local incumbent CRMs** (Zid CRM, regional Salesforce SIs) — CRM-only,
   no lead-generation engine, no AI scoring.
3. **Lead-list / agency model** — per-list cost, no compounding asset,
   no audit trail, breaks under PDPL scrutiny.
4. **Status quo** (manual + spreadsheets) — does not scale, BDR burnout,
   no governance.

Dealix counter: "We are the engine that fills your CRM with PDPL-
compliant, vertical-tagged, ranked Saudi leads — and the operating
partner that turns them into outcomes." Full detail in
`docs/strategy/competitive_landscape_sa.md`.

## Addressable opportunity (Dealix bottom-up)

- **Tier 1 enterprise core** (BFSI / Telco / Energy / Govt-adjacent /
  Large Retail / Healthcare networks): ~800 named accounts, SAR 500K–3M
  ARR each → SAR 0.4–2.4B TAM.
- **Tier 2 mid-market**: ~6,000 firms (SAR 30M–200M revenue) → SAR
  600M–3B TAM at SAR 100K–500K ARR each.
- **Tier 3 SME**: 700K+ VAT-registered B2B SMEs at SAR 24K–100K ARR
  each → very large but PLG/long-tail.
