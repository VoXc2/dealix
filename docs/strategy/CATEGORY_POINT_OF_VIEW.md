## Context

A category is not a market segment. It is a worldview the buyer
agrees with **before** they pick a vendor. This document is the
positioning manifesto Dealix commits to publicly. It anchors
everything in `docs/company/DEALIX_CEO_STRATEGY.md`,

## The Old World

> Companies buy AI tools and run **disconnected experiments**.

They license chatbots, scrape leads, test agents, demo prompts, and
generate decks. The output is movement, not value. Models change
monthly; tools come and go. Most "AI projects" do not change a
business number.

## The Problem

AI tools do **not** create value unless they are connected to:

- Clean data the company actually has.
- A workflow the output flows into.
- Human approval for external actions.
- Governance that maps to local law (in Saudi: PDPL).
- Proof that ties the change to a business metric.

Without these, AI is theater. With them, AI is a capability.

## The New World

> Companies need **AI Operating Systems**, not AI tools.

The buyer's question shifts from *"Which AI tool do I license?"* to
*"What is the governed AI operating layer for my company?"*

That shift is the category.

## Dealix Belief

> **AI is not a tool. AI is an operating capability.**

A capability has: an owner, an input, a workflow, an approval, an
output, a metric, an audit, and a cadence. A tool has none of these
by default. Vendors selling tools cannot deliver capabilities.
Vendors selling capabilities cannot do it without an operating
system underneath.

## The Category

> **Arabic-first AI Operations for Saudi and MENA companies.**

The category has four anchors:

- **Arabic-first** — register, localization, quality scoring,
  governance phrasing.
- **AI Operations** — capabilities, not tools; cadence, not
  experiments.
- **Saudi/MENA** — PDPL, Vision 2030, partner ecosystem, local
  proof.
- **Operating system** — the layer above LLMs, productized.

## Who This Is For (and Not For)

For:

- Saudi/MENA mid-market and enterprise leaders who already see AI
  as inevitable and want governed adoption.
- Operators who measure outcomes, not tool spend.
- Compliance officers who need approvals, audits, and PDPL fit.

Not for:

- Buyers chasing the latest model headlines.
- Buyers seeking guaranteed revenue uplifts.
- Buyers unwilling to share data sources or assign a sponsor.

## What This Changes In The Pitch

Old pitch: "We build AI solutions."
New pitch: **"We are the governed AI operating layer for your
company. We do not sell you AI. We make AI a measurable capability
inside your operations."**

Old proof: features and demos.
New proof: Proof Packs, capital ledger entries, audit logs.

Old buyer: VP of Innovation.
New buyer: COO, Head of RevOps, Head of Compliance, CEO.
