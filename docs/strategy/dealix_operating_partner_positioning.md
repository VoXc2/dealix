# Dealix AI Operating Partner — Company Positioning

## 1. Context

Dealix should not be positioned as "an AI tool" or "a lead intelligence product." The market is saturated with AI features that companies adopt without seeing measurable outcomes. The Saudi enterprise opportunity is **operational**: companies want AI to actually run parts of their business — and they are willing to pay for outcome-bearing services, not generic AI access.

The positioning is therefore:

> **Dealix is the AI Operating Partner for Saudi companies.**

We sell operational results — revenue, time, clarity, quality, governance — not "AI capability." Every offering is tied to one of five customer-facing outcomes and powered by one of eight internal OS modules.

This document is the company-level positioning statement, supersedes the legacy "Revenue OS only" framing, and demotes the Lead Engine (T31) from "the company" to "the highest-priority service line within the Grow Revenue pillar."

## 2. Audience

- **Customer**: 30-second elevator answer ("we run AI operations for Saudi companies in 5 outcome areas").
- **Partner / channel**: catalog overview of where to plug in.
- **Internal**: product, engineering, sales, marketing alignment on what we sell and what we build.

## 3. Decisions / Content

### 3.1 The thesis

1. **Companies don't pay for AI. They pay for results.** Time saved, revenue gained, errors reduced, customers served, decisions made.
2. **AI adoption ≠ AI impact.** Most Saudi enterprises have used AI; few have moved a KPI with it. The gap is operations, data, and use-case discipline — not models.
3. **Dealix's wedge** is being the operating partner that owns the gap: choosing the right use case, fixing the data, building the workflow, measuring the result, governing the risk.
4. **One brand, five outcome pillars.** Customer sees outcomes; engineering builds shared OS modules underneath; CRO sells outcomes; CS delivers outcomes.

### 3.2 Five customer-facing pillars

1. **Grow Revenue** — sales pipeline, lead intelligence, outreach, conversion. (Houses T31 Saudi Lead Engine.)
2. **Serve Customers** — AI support, inbox/WhatsApp operations, feedback intelligence.
3. **Automate Operations** — workflow automation, executive reporting, SOP automation.
4. **Build Company Brain** — knowledge base / RAG, sales assistant, policy assistant.
5. **Govern AI** — AI readiness, usage policy, PDPL-aware data review, audit logs.

### 3.3 Eight internal OS modules (the engine room)

| # | Module | Powers pillars |
|---|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Intake OS | all |
| 2 | Data Quality OS | Grow, Serve, Operations, Brain |
| 3 | Scoring OS | Grow, Operations |
| 4 | Workflow OS | Operations, Serve |
| 5 | Knowledge OS | Brain, Serve |
| 6 | Outreach OS | Grow, Serve |
| 7 | Reporting OS | all |
| 8 | Governance OS | all (mandatory for every action) |

Each pillar's offerings are concrete products composed from these modules. New service offerings must reuse existing modules — no module proliferation per offering.

### 3.4 What we do NOT do (anti-positioning)

- We do not sell "an AI subscription" with vague benefits.
- We do not replace human judgment in hiring, lending, or clinical decisions.
- We do not generate spam outreach without consent (PDPL Art. 13/14 enforced).
- We do not run analytics-only engagements without an attached action.
- We do not customize per-customer without productizing the recurring pattern.

### 3.5 Customer 30-second pitch

> "Dealix is the AI Operating Partner for Saudi companies. We don't sell AI tools — we run five outcome programs: grow your revenue, serve your customers, automate your operations, build your company brain, and govern your AI. Every program is delivered as a fixed-scope SAR-priced sprint that ships in 1–4 weeks, with measurable outcomes and a PDPL-compliant audit trail."

### 3.6 Investor / partner 60-second pitch

> "Saudi enterprises spend ~SAR 4–8 billion / year on consulting and SaaS to digitize. Most never see ROI because they buy capabilities, not outcomes. Dealix is the first AI-native operating partner that productizes outcomes — 30+ services across 5 pillars, all powered by 8 reusable OS modules, all PDPL-compliant. We compound: every sprint produces a customer dataset that improves the next sprint. We are not a CRM, we are not a chatbot — we are the operating partner buyers wish their consulting firm had become."

### 3.7 Three-phase rollout

- **Phase 1 (Months 1–6) — Cash services**: short-cycle, paid sprints (Revenue Diagnostic, Lead Intelligence, AI Quick Win, Support Desk). Goal: 20–30 paying customers, prove repeatability per pillar.
- **Phase 2 (Months 7–12) — Retainer services**: monthly programs (Monthly RevOps, Monthly AI Ops, Customer Support AI, Reporting Automation). Goal: 15+ retainers, predictable MRR.
- **Phase 3 (Year 2+) — Enterprise services**: Company Brain, Enterprise AI OS, AI Governance Program, multi-BU rollouts. Goal: 5+ enterprise customers, SAR 1M+ ARR per logo.

### 3.8 Demoting T31 within the new frame

T31 Saudi Lead Engine remains the highest-RICE service line — but as the **flagship offering inside Grow Revenue**, not the whole company. The Master Plan (W0) is updated accordingly. Engineering rigor (ADRs, SLO, FinOps, policy engine) developed for T31 transfers to all eight OS modules.

## 4. KPIs

- Revenue from each pillar tracked separately starting Month 7.
- At least 3 of 5 pillars active (≥1 paying customer each) by Month 9.
- No pillar > 70% of total revenue by end of Year 2 (diversification).
- Productized service ratio: ≥ 80% of revenue from listed catalog offerings (not bespoke custom work) by Month 12.
