## Context
Dealix's strategic destination is to own a category in MENA — "The
Dealix Standard for AI Operations." Reaching that destination requires
five disciplined stages, not a leap. This file defines those stages and
the rule that we never advance before the previous stage is stable. It is
the operating spine for category design and connects directly to
`docs/V14_COMPREHENSIVE_STRATEGIC_PLAN.md`,
`docs/COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING.md`, and
`docs/strategic/DEALIX_MASTER_OPERATING_MODEL_AR.md`.

## The five stages

1. **Service** — deliver value manually with templates.
   - Win clients, sign small engagements, prove value by hand.
   - Outputs: proof packs, signed ledger entries, satisfied first clients.

2. **System** — turn delivery into a repeatable workflow.
   - The same delivery, faster, with checklists and clear owners.
   - Outputs: workflow documents, SOPs, training materials.

3. **Software** — automate repeated steps.
   - The repeating manual steps become product features and agents.
   - Outputs: deployed agents, the AI Control Tower, productized data.

4. **Standard** — publish the Dealix Method and quality benchmarks.
   - The method becomes a public standard with benchmarks and rules.
   - Outputs: governance standard, quality benchmarks, public docs.

5. **Ecosystem** — train partners, certify practitioners, expand MENA.
   - The method spreads through agencies, partners, and certifications.
   - Outputs: certification program, partner playbooks, regional presence.

## The non-advance rule

> Do not move to a stage before the previous one is **stable**.

Stable means:

- The current stage is repeatable across at least three clients/projects.
- Margins are positive or strategically acceptable.
- Quality is at threshold consistently.
- Risk events are not repeating.

## Decision lens for the leadership team

Each quarter, the leadership team asks:

1. Are we stable at the current stage?
2. What blocks advancing to the next stage?
3. Which investments collapse the next blocker?
4. Are we tempted to skip a stage? Why?

## Anti-patterns

- Building Software before the System exists.
- Publishing a Standard before the Method works.
- Recruiting Ecosystem partners before there is a Standard to teach.
- Returning to "Service" forever to avoid the discipline of System.

## Metrics
- Stage Stability Score — composite of repeatability, margin, QA, risk.
- Time-in-Stage — months at current stage.
- Premature Advance — count of advances rolled back.
- Standard Adoption — public adopters of the Dealix Standard.
