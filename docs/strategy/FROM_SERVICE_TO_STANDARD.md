# From Service to Standard — The 5-Stage Evolution

> Dealix's long-term moat is not the services it sells today. It is the
> **standard** it becomes for AI Operations in Saudi and MENA. The path
> from service company → industry standard has 5 stages. Skipping a stage
> kills the company.

## Stage 1 — Service

**Where Dealix is today**: 3 productized Sellable services + Diagnostic gateway.

**Characteristics**:
- Services are productized but execution is mostly human-led.
- Templates exist but are filled by hand.
- Quality depends on the founder.

**Goal**: prove the services repeatedly. Don't skip ahead.
**Gate to next stage**: 3+ paying customers per service with Quality Score ≥ 85 average.

## Stage 2 — System

**What it looks like**:
- Delivery is checklist-driven (anyone with the checklist can run it).
- QA gates fire automatically.
- Ledgers (`docs/ledgers/`) capture every project.
- Capability Operating Model is live; customers see their capability roadmap.

**Goal**: turn delivery into a repeatable workflow that doesn't depend on the founder.
**Gate to next stage**: another team member delivers a full project independently to Quality Score ≥ 85.

## Stage 3 — Software

**What it looks like**:
- The internal OS modules automate the repeated steps (data quality scoring, report generation, proof pack assembly).
- Customer workspace ships (skeletal frontend).
- Service-assisted SaaS becomes the dominant revenue mix.
- LLM Gateway live; cost per project is bounded.

**Goal**: software absorbs the repetitive work; humans focus on judgment.
**Gate to next stage**: 10+ paid projects with software-assisted delivery; LLM cost per project < 30% of revenue per project.

## Stage 4 — Standard

**What it looks like**:
- Dealix Method published externally (selected portions).
- Quality benchmarks (`docs/quality/QUALITY_STANDARD.md`) become category reference.
- Annual "Saudi AI Operations Benchmark" published with anonymized data from real deliveries.
- Customers cite Dealix Standard in their own RFPs.

**Goal**: Dealix becomes the way Saudi companies talk about AI Operations.
**Gate to next stage**: 25+ paid customers; 3+ published anonymized case studies; 1 sectoral playbook publicly available.

## Stage 5 — Ecosystem

**What it looks like**:
- Dealix Academy trains practitioners.
- Partners can deliver Dealix-certified Sprints under quality oversight.
- Multiple agencies / consultancies use Dealix as their AI-Ops backbone.
- MENA expansion (UAE, Bahrain, Kuwait) follows established Saudi playbooks.

**Goal**: the category itself runs on Dealix infrastructure.
**No further "gate"** — Stage 5 is a maturity state, not a checkpoint.

## Anti-pattern: skipping stages

| Attempted skip | What dies |
|----------------|-----------|
| Service → Software directly | Engineering builds features without proof of paid demand; cash burns; quality drops |
| System → Standard directly | Standard claims without enough customer proof = marketing fiction; reputation hit |
| Software → Ecosystem directly | Partners deliver chaos under your brand without quality oversight infrastructure |
| Stage 1 → Stage 4 in a year | Company collapses under its own ambition |

## Capital growth per stage

- Stage 1: Service Capital + Trust Capital (first proof packs).
- Stage 2: Knowledge Capital + Service Capital depth.
- Stage 3: Product Capital + Knowledge Capital depth.
- Stage 4: Market Capital + Trust Capital at scale.
- Stage 5: All 5 capitals reinforcing each other.

## Owner & cadence

- **Owner**: CEO (this is the company's strategic arc).
- **Review**: quarterly. Promotion to next stage requires explicit board (or self) sign-off + evidence per the gate above.
