# Dealix Decision Rule

A unified six-question filter applied to every meaningful decision — a new offer, a new feature, a new market, a new partner, a new venture.

## 1. The six questions

1. **Does it sell?**
2. **Does it deliver?**
3. **Does it prove?**
4. **Does it govern?**
5. **Does it compound?**
6. **Does it scale?**

بالعربي:

1. هل يبيع؟
2. هل نستطيع تسليمه؟
3. هل يثبت قيمة؟
4. هل يمكن حوكمته؟
5. هل يراكم أصلًا؟
6. هل يمكن توسيعه؟

## 2. Scoring band

| Yeses | Verdict |
| --- | --- |
| 0–2 | Do not act |
| 3 | Act with caution |
| 4 | Priority |
| 5–6 | Strategic bet |

## 4. Operating discipline

- Every Decision Log entry cites the six-question score.
- A `Strategic Bet` requires a Capital Plan; a `Priority` requires a kill-criteria addendum.
- A `Caution` decision triggers a 30-day follow-up review.
- A `Do not act` decision is logged with the reason and the time horizon under which it might be reconsidered.

## 5. Anti-patterns

- Scoring decisions verbally and not writing them down.
- Awarding "yes" to a question without a piece of evidence.
- Letting one strong "yes" carry the rest of the decision.
- Reusing yesterday's score for today's decision.

## 6. The principle

> Six honest yeses beat any one convincing argument.
