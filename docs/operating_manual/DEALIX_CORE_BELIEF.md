# Dealix Core Belief

> AI alone does not create value. AI creates value when it is attached to data, workflow, human approval, governance, QA, proof, and operating cadence.
>
> بالعربي: AI وحده لا يخلق قيمة. AI يخلق قيمة عندما يدخل في بيانات واضحة، workflow محدد، مراجعة بشرية، حوكمة، جودة، proof، وتشغيل مستمر.

## 1. The seven attachments

1. **Data** — sourced, passported, classified.
2. **Workflow** — owned, scoped, instrumented.
3. **Human approval** — at the points where it matters.
4. **Governance** — evaluated at runtime, not in a PDF.
5. **QA** — independent of the operator who built it.
6. **Proof** — Proof Pack as the closing artifact.
7. **Operating cadence** — recurring review and refresh.

When all seven are present, AI is an operating capability. When even one is missing, AI is a demo.

## 2. Use of the belief

- It is the **filter** applied to every new offer, sprint, or feature.
- It is the **opening statement** for every senior buyer conversation.
- It is the **closing statement** of every proposal and case study.
- It is the **bar** against which "is this AI?" arguments are settled.

## 3. Anti-patterns

- Selling models as if they were the value.
- Pilots without workflow ownership.
- Outputs that bypass human approval at the moments that matter.
- Governance that exists only on a slide.
- QA performed by the same operator who built the output.
- Claims without a Proof Pack ID.
- Engagements without an operating cadence after delivery.

## 4. The principle

> Without the seven attachments, AI is theater. With them, AI is capability.
