## Context
Dealix sells AI operations work to MENA buyers who have been promised
miracles before and got vapor. The only defensible sales motion in
this context is proof-based: every claim made in a conversation is
backed by an artifact a buyer can see, touch, or download. This file
codifies how Dealix builds and sequences that proof, before, during,
and after a customer. It plugs into the revenue playbook in
`docs/DEALIX_REVENUE_PLAYBOOK_FINAL.md`.

## The Principle

> Don't sell with promises. Sell with proof.

A promise is "we can improve your lead quality." Proof is "here is a
sample of how we re-scored a comparable account, anonymized, with the
delta in pipeline value annotated." The first is a hope. The second is
a thing the buyer can hold.

## Proof Maturity by Phase

### Before any case studies — Phase 0
You have not closed a customer yet. You build proof anyway:

- **Sample proof pack** — anonymized, fictional but realistic.
- **Demo CSV** — a representative dataset re-scored or enriched.
- **Sample report** — a one-page executive summary of a fictional engagement.
- **Before / after mock example** — what an account looked like before and after.

These are produced once and reused across every Phase 0 conversation.

### After the first customer — Phase 1
You have one shipped engagement:

- **Anonymized proof pack** — derived from the real engagement, all
  identifiers removed.
- **Case-style post** — a published narrative, no PII, no logo unless
  permission was granted in writing.
- **Numbers without exposing the client** — directional metrics with
  ranges, not exact values that could re-identify.

### After three customers — Phase 2
You have a vertical view:

- **Vertical benchmark** — anonymized comparison across the three
  customers.
- **Common problems report** — the patterns observed across the
  segment.
- **Industry playbook** — your defensible point of view, derived from
  the data.

## The Three Rules

| # | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1 | No fake proof — never fabricate an artifact, a number, or a quote. |
| 2 | No guaranteed results — never promise an outcome you cannot control. |
| 3 | No invented numbers — every metric traces to a delivery file. |

A breach of any rule is grounds for pulling the asset from circulation
and escalating to the founder.

## Sales Sequencing

In a sales conversation:

1. Lead with the highest-maturity proof available for the buyer's
   vertical.
2. If no vertical proof exists, lead with the closest adjacent proof
   and name the difference.
3. Never lead with a slide. Always lead with an artifact.
4. End with one CTA (see `docs/client/CLIENT_JOURNEY.md`).

- Proof artifacts produced per delivery — target ≥ 1.
- Proof-led conversations — % of sales calls that led with an artifact.
- Time from delivery to anonymized proof — target ≤ 14 days.
- Rule breach count — target 0.
