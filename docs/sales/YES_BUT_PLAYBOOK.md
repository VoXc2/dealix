# Yes-But Playbook

> Hard "no" loses the deal. Hard "yes" violates Dealix Standard. The right
> answer is almost always "yes, but in this safer / scoped form". This
> playbook codifies the redirect.

## Pattern

1. **Acknowledge the underlying need** ("you want to reach decision-makers fast").
2. **Name the constraint** ("WhatsApp without consent is PDPL-blocked and Dealix Standard 3 blocks it").
3. **Offer the safer equivalent** ("we deliver bilingual outreach drafts; you approve and send via your normal channel").
4. **Quantify the safer path** (price, timeline, expected outcome).

This keeps the prospect's intent alive while protecting the standard.

## The 12 most common asks (and the Yes-But response)

| Customer ask | Yes-But response | Sellable alternative |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| "Send WhatsApp to all my leads" | Drafts ready, customer approves per-contact, consent confirmed | Lead Intelligence Sprint outputs + outbound consultancy |
| "Scrape competitor websites for leads" | Research-only from public registries (MoC CR, Maroof, chambers) with attribution | Lead Intel with Saudi-native sources |
| "Guarantee 20% pipeline growth" | We commit to deliverables, not market outcomes; success criteria measurable but bounded | Sprint SOW with explicit deliverables |
| "Just send everything in 3 days" | Fix-scope sprint with a 7/10/21-day timeline; rushed scope = quality failure | Choose AI Quick Win (7d) if scope fits |
| "Use my customer's WhatsApp numbers for marketing" | Allowed only with documented PDPL Art. 14 consent; otherwise blocked | Re-engagement campaign through your own consented channels |
| "Build us a complete enterprise AI system" | Start with Diagnostic + Sprint; promote to Enterprise after gate met | AI Ops Diagnostic → Sprint → Pilot → Retainer → Enterprise |
| "Use cold leads from a list I bought" | If lawful basis is documented; otherwise research-only | Lead Intelligence Sprint with seed normalization |
| "We need this without governance gates" | Governance is non-removable; we can streamline approvals but not bypass | Streamlined approval matrix variant |
| "Just give me access to ChatGPT" | We sell operating systems, not access; alternative: AI Quick Win to automate a real workflow | AI Quick Win Sprint |
| "Pay only on results" | Outcome-based contracts only at Enterprise tier (revenue-share band per `docs/pricing/value_metrics.md`) | Sprint at fixed price; revenue-share at Enterprise |
| "Sign now, scope later" | We start with paid Diagnostic to lock scope | Revenue Diagnostic (SAR 3,500–7,500) |
| "Build us the same as company X" | We don't copy; we deliver per your business. Sample anonymized Proof Pack shows our outcomes | Diagnostic → Sprint |

## When the Yes-But fails to redirect

If the customer insists on the forbidden path:
1. CRO sign-off required to continue any conversation.
2. Politely document the no in writing: "we cannot do X because Y; here is our alternative; we're happy to talk if Z."
3. Add to the lessons-learned in `docs/sales/objection_handling.md`.

## Owner & cadence
- **Owner**: CRO + sales team train on this playbook monthly.
- **Update**: every new "weird ask" the team hears gets added to the table within 7 days.
