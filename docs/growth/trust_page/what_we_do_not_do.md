## Context
Trust is established by drawing a clear line around what Dealix refuses to
do. This page is the public version of those refusals. Each line is
backed by the runtime rules in
`docs/product/GOVERNANCE_AS_CODE.md` and the operating posture in
`docs/DEALIX_OPERATING_CONSTITUTION.md`.

## We do not

- We do **not** build spam systems.
- We do **not** support cold WhatsApp automation.
- We do **not** support LinkedIn automation.
- We do **not** use unsourced personal data.
- We do **not** create fake proof.
- We do **not** promise guaranteed sales.
- We do **not** deliver source-less knowledge answers.

## Why each refusal exists

- **Spam systems** harm long-term sender reputation and violate norms.
- **Cold WhatsApp** breaches consent expectations in MENA and abroad.
- **LinkedIn automation** breaks platform terms and creates fragile pipes.
- **Unsourced personal data** lacks lawful basis under PDPL and similar
  frameworks.
- **Fake proof** destroys the only currency Dealix sells — trust.
- **Guaranteed sales** are non-falsifiable and harm reputation.
- **Source-less knowledge** cannot be audited or trusted.

## What we do instead

- Consent-based, segmented business email.
- Approved-account outreach via direct relationships.
- Manual, governed enrichment with documented lawful basis.
- Proof packs traceable to inputs, prompts, and reviewers.
- Range-based outcome modeling instead of guarantees.
- Cited answers, or honest "insufficient evidence."

## Enforcement

These commitments are enforced by:

- Compliance Guard Agent rules.
- Eval suites in `docs/EVALS_RUNBOOK.md`.
- Audit logs surfaced in the AI Control Tower.
- Public commitments on this page.
