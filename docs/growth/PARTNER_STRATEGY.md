## Context
Dealix is the AI operations layer for established service providers in
adjacent categories. Partner Strategy declares the categories Dealix
prefers to partner with, the engagement models that govern those
partnerships, and the maturity gate before each model is allowed. It
implements the partner stance from
`docs/AGENCY_PARTNER_PROGRAM.md` and
`docs/business/AGENCY_PARTNER_REVENUE_MODEL.md`, and the operational
playbook in `docs/growth/AGENCY_RESELLER_PLAYBOOK.md`.

## Preferred partner categories

- **CRM consultants** — Their clients already have data; Dealix
  layers AI operations on top.
- **Accounting and ERP firms** — Their clients have systems but
  not AI workflow; Dealix plugs in cleanly.
- **Marketing agencies** — Their clients need lead intelligence and
  content systems; Dealix is the operational engine.
- **Cybersecurity consultants** — Their clients have governance
  posture; Dealix completes the AI-specific layer.
- **Training companies** — Their reach lets Dealix Academy scale.
- **Business consultants** — Their strategy work creates the demand
  Dealix delivers against.

Across all of these Dealix occupies one consistent position: the AI
operations layer of the partner's client.

## Engagement models

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| Referral fee | Partner introduces; Dealix delivers; fee paid on closed-won. |
| Revenue share | Partner co-sells; ongoing share of recurring revenue. |
| White-label sprint | Sprint delivered under partner brand; Dealix invisible to the end client. |
| Joint delivery | Partner and Dealix run the engagement together; both visible. |

## The maturity gate

> Do not enter white-label until delivery quality is stable.

White-label is the highest-trust model and exposes the partner's
brand to Dealix execution. It is allowed only when sprint quality is
demonstrably consistent across at least five completed projects, with
proof packs that meet the standard described in
`docs/business/MANAGED_PILOT_OFFER.md` and the trust criteria of the
Capital Model.

Until then, partnerships start at Referral, can move to Revenue Share
on demonstrated co-selling, and may consider Joint Delivery on a case
basis.

## Partner economics

- **Referral** typically 10–15% of first-year revenue.
- **Revenue share** typically 15–25% of recurring revenue while
  active.
- **White-label** priced as a wholesale sprint with the partner
  responsible for client-facing presentation.
- **Joint delivery** scoped per engagement with explicit role split.

Final percentages are negotiated and recorded in the partner contract;
defaults are reviewed annually.

## Metrics
- Active partners — count of partners with at least one closed-won deal in last 6 months; target ≥ 5 by year-end.
- Partner-sourced revenue share — share of new revenue sourced through partners; target ≥ 25%.
- White-label readiness — number of services that have cleared the maturity gate; target ≥ 2.
- Co-sell win rate — share of partner-sourced opportunities won; target ≥ 30%.
