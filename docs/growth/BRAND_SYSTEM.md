## Context
The Brand System declares the message, pillars, anti-position, and
voice that hold every Dealix surface together — sales, product,
content, partner. It is the upstream constraint for
`docs/BRAND_PRESS_KIT.md`, the visual standards in
`docs/DEALIX_DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md`, and the differentiation work in
`docs/COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING.md`.

## Core message

- **English** AI from experiment to operating system.
- **Arabic** نحوّل الذكاء الاصطناعي من أفكار وتجارب إلى أنظمة تشغيل
  داخل الشركات.

The message is parallel-language, not translated. It is the single
sentence that every other piece of brand copy must be consistent with.

## Six brand pillars

1. **Data readiness** — Most AI fails because the data isn't ready;
   Dealix starts there.
2. **Workflow design** — AI is plugged into workflows, not chats.
3. **Human approval** — Humans approve outcomes; AI proposes,
   accelerates, drafts.
4. **Governance** — Controls, audit logs, and risk policy are part of
   delivery from day one.
5. **Proof of impact** — Every project produces a proof pack tied to a
   measurable KPI.
6. **Arabic-first quality** — Arabic content meets executive
   standards, not translation standards.

These pillars appear in every sales conversation, every public asset,
and every internal review.

## Anti-position

What Dealix is **not**:

- **Spam automation** — We do not build outbound spam machines.
- **Chatbot agency** — We do not deploy bots without process design.
- **Generic AI consultant** — We do not advise on AI in the abstract.

Each anti-position is enforced in proposal screening, content review,
and partner qualification. Engagements that drift into any of these
categories are reshaped or declined.

## Voice and style

- **Clear** — Plain language, no jargon for jargon's sake.
- **Executive** — Written for decision makers, not specialists.
- **Precise** — Numbers, KPIs, and proof are stated, not implied.
- **Not hyped** — No "transform your business" language.
- **Evidence-based** — Claims travel with proof; otherwise they are
  hypotheses.

## Voice tests

Before any external piece ships, it is checked against three filters:

1. Does it state a measurable outcome?
2. Could a peer say the same thing? If yes, sharpen.
3. Would an executive forward it to a colleague? If not, rewrite.

## Metrics
- Pillar coverage — share of public assets covering ≥ 2 pillars; target ≥ 90%.
- Anti-drift incidents — public pieces flagged as drifting into spam / bot / generic territory; target 0.
- Voice-test pass rate — share of public pieces passing all three voice filters on first review; target ≥ 80%.
- Bilingual parity — share of public assets shipped with EN + AR parity; target 100%.
