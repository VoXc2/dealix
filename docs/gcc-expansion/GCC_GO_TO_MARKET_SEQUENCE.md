# GCC Go-To-Market Sequence

## Sequence Logic
Each step must be true before the next step activates. Skipping a step is a doctrine violation.

## Step 1 — Saudi Proof
- Invoice #1 paid by a Saudi B2B services or regulated-adjacent buyer.
- One Proof Pack, one Value Ledger entry, one Capital Asset, one retainer recommendation.

## Step 2 — Saudi Repeatability
- At least 2 paying retainers active.
- Revenue Intelligence Sprint delivered at least twice.
- Documented delivery checklist usable by a hired operator.

## Step 3 — Anchor Partner Activation
- At least one signed anchor partner agreement.
- Rev-share and Partner Covenant documented and signed.
- One co-sold deal in the Saudi market via the partner.

## Step 4 — First Expansion Market (UAE)
Trigger requires all four:
- Saudi proof + repeatability complete (Steps 1-2).
- Signed anchor partner with UAE reach.
- One named UAE buyer with budget and timeline.
- Data residency and regulatory review documented.

## Step 5 — Second-Wave Markets (Qatar, Bahrain)
Trigger requires all four:
- One UAE invoice paid.
- One UAE retainer active.
- Anchor partner agreement for the target market.
- Saudi case study with verified proof.

## Step 6 — Long-Tail Markets (Kuwait, Oman)
Activation only after at least one of:
- Direct inbound from a named buyer with budget.
- Partner-led intro with signed scope.

## Anti-Patterns Banned By Doctrine
- announcing a regional launch before Saudi proof
- staffing in expansion markets before retainer motion is proven
- buying broad ad inventory in expansion markets
- spinning up self-service signup outside Saudi
- publishing case studies without verified proof
- using a single Saudi success to claim regional momentum
